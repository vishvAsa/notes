There is a quote attributed to the German mathematician David Hilbert (it is often mentioned, but nobody knows if he really said it or not): 

> "Every boy in the streets of Göttingen understands more about four-dimensional geometry than Einstein. Yet, in spite of that, Einstein did the work and not the mathematicians."

Hilbert, on hearing of Einstein's work, tried to arrive at general relativity using the variational principle; however, he failed to reach the famous field equations whose form he learnt only after Einstein discovered them.

Similarly, in the tamiL country, a lot of v1s know the taittirIya shruti, but few know the way of the Aryan.  
In other circles, everyone spouts paNini from the first to the last sUtra and backward and from anywhere in the middle, but few know the Arya-rahasya. That is the way of knowledge -- special or inner understanding can come from him who may not be the most agile scholar in the field but the most discerning.
