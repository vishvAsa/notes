The *famous* biologist Robert Trivers has died. His theoretical contributions were v.influential to the generation after him. However, it is also notable that he was an acolyte and strong supporter of the notorious Apastiya. 

While Trivers should have continued to be respected for his contributions (like I believe all scientists should be after their prime), an interesting  Trivers-boosterism appeared suddenly. He was being called things like "the greatest biologist after Darwin", "the greatest living biologist"... This boosterism has been little analyzed in the closed circles of academia. 

We believe this process closely paralleled the boosterism of his friend Apastiya himself and was likely orchestrated from within those circles. An extended version of that still exists in academia and still exerts a rather pernicious effect on science that cannot be seen by outsiders. There is another facet to it, which is a bit too dangerous to air in public, for those in power are still in power.
