Six Key Issues Regarding the Tongzhi Hui Rebellion (Revised Edition)

Foreword

Human history, from ancient times to the present, is a continuous process of creating tragedies, then forgetting them, and then repeating the same tragedies over and over again.

There should be . There are always some who want us to deliberately remember some parts of history while deliberately forgetting others, claiming it’s for the sake of a better present life. This is truly laughable. If we can’t even face history squarely, how can we face reality squarely? How can we possibly live a good life in the present?

Before we begin our discussion, let’s clarify the difference between facts and opinions . “Today’s temperature is 28℃” and “You are 180cm tall” are facts; “It’s quite hot today” and “You’re quite tall” are opinions.

Facts are the raw material for our discussion. We verify these facts through various sources to obtain relatively reliable historical data, then reflect on and evaluate this data to arrive at our own opinions. It’s important to note that everyone’s opinion is subjective , and history has no standard answers. We need to question, identify, and think critically before drawing our own judgments .

If you agree with the above viewpoints, please continue reading. If you believe you represent perfection and objectivity, please stay away from this place.

The following discussion concerns an extremely tragic period in modern Chinese history.

The Forgotten Religious Genocide

In 1937, the Japanese army massacred 300,000 Chinese people in Nanjing.

The Rwandan genocide in the 1990s killed one million people.

In 1915, Türkiye massacred 1.3 million Armenians.

Nazi Germany murdered 6 million Jews in the 1940s.

But 150 years ago, China experienced one of the most serious religious genocides in modern world history. The number of Han Chinese killed in the massacre was 66 times that of the Nanjing Massacre and more than 3 times that of the Nazi Holocaust . This was the Tongzhi Hui Rebellion 150 years ago.

The Tongzhi Hui Rebellion was a large-scale upheaval and war that occurred in Northwest China during the Tongzhi reign of the Qing Dynasty. The conflict was primarily a genocidal massacre perpetrated by Hui Muslims against Han Chinese non-Muslims . Taking advantage of the Qing central government’s preoccupation with suppressing the Taiping Rebellion and its inability to attend to matters in the west, the Hui Muslims attempted to establish a highly autonomous Islamic government, or even an independent Islamic state, in the Shaanxi-Gansu region.

The Tongzhi Hui Rebellion lasted eleven years, from 1862 to 1873, affecting provinces such as Shaanxi, Gansu, Ningxia, Qinghai, and Xinjiang. It caused immense damage to the economy and culture of Northwest China and was ultimately suppressed by the central government army, primarily the Hunan Army. Historical records indicate that an estimated 20 million Han Chinese were massacred.

Because of several regime changes in modern times, many people are unaware of this great human tragedy, and many viewpoints are distorted and confused due to various reasons. To clarify the issue, the following analysis will examine it from six perspectives:

Qing oppression?

1. Was the Shaanxi Hui Muslim riot a rebellion against the oppression of the Qing government?

In its treatment of ethnic relations, the Qing government arranged the ethnic groups as “Manchu, Mongol, Tibetan, Hui, Han,” placing the Hui people before the Han people. Both Hui and Han people were considered to be at the bottom of society. The Hui people in Shaanxi and Gansu were mostly engaged in small-scale commerce and did not smoke opium. In the rural Guanzhong Plain at that time, the Hui people were wealthier than the Han people. The Hui people have historically been skilled in martial arts and formed organized groups; even in conflicts between the Han and Hui, the Han people usually suffered losses.

The logic is simple: during the Qing Dynasty, both Han and Hui people were oppressed. If the Hui Muslim uprising was seen as a rebellion against Qing oppression, then the perpetrators would naturally seek revenge against the oppressors, attacking the Qing army instead of indiscriminately massacring innocent Han civilians. Clearly, anti-Qing sentiment was a slogan; the true purpose was the massacre of Han people. The Hui Muslim forces started in the countryside, killing Han people indiscriminately, regardless of age or gender. After wiping out the rural Han population, they then besieged county towns and provincial capitals, aiming to exterminate all Han people in Shaanxi and Gansu provinces. How did the perpetrators distinguish friend from foe? By religion. Anyone not a Muslim, regardless of age or gender, was killed. The Hui Muslims’ genocide was a thousand times worse than the Qing government’s oppression. What kind of resistance against Qing oppression is this?!

How many crimes have been committed openly in the name of resisting tyranny? How many criminals of nations and peoples have appeared on the historical stage disguised as rebels? Sun Yat-sen, in his determination to overthrow the Qing government by any means necessary , even portrayed the perpetrators of genocide as heroes leading the anti-Qing movement.

In his book *A History of the Soul*, Hui writer Zhang Chengzhi only recounts the tragic history of the Hui people’s suppression, glorifying the bloodthirsty butchers Ma Hualong and Bai Yanhu as national heroes resisting tyranny, while omitting the history of the Hui people’s frenzied massacres of Han Chinese. What kind of values are these? Without the initial cause of the Muslim massacres, there would have been no subsequent violent suppression by the central army. Intellectuals should provide comprehensive information and the logical cause and effect of events. It’s like the atomic bomb memorial in Japan; the museum repeatedly states how many citizens of Hiroshima and Nagasaki suffered during the bombings, but never reflects on why the Allied forces dropped atomic bombs on them. Without the initial cause of Japanese militaristic expansion, there would have been no subsequent consequences of the atomic bombs.

The core of the incident lies in the fact that Hui Muslims launched a holy war in an attempt to establish a purely Islamic state in Northwest China and carry out genocide against non-Muslim Han Chinese.

Note: The Yunnan Hui Rebellion came first, followed by the Shaanxi-Gansu Tongzhi Hui Rebellion. Before the Shaanxi-Gansu Hui Rebellion, Yunnan Muslims had been launching rebellions for decades, starting in 1818, with the aim of establishing an Islamic state. Historical records confirm that the Shaanxi-Gansu Hui Rebellion was also initiated by Yunnan Muslims in Shaanxi.

Han loss cause

II. Why did the Han Chinese suffer such heavy casualties?

In 1861, before the massacre, Shaanxi had a total population of 13.94 million, of which Hui people accounted for about 12% .

Although the Hui Muslims were outnumbered, they initially controlled almost all of Shaanxi and Gansu except for Lanzhou, carrying out a genocidal massacre of Han Chinese within this region. The main reasons for the immense losses suffered by the Han people are as follows:

1. Muslims share a strong religious identity, practice a theocratic system, and possess a powerful ability to unite as a group. Following the Hui Muslim riots in Shaanxi, Hui Muslims in Gansu, Ningxia, and other regions responded accordingly.

2. The Hui people, relying on the religious organizations, had a strong military mobilization capability, and their people could be gathered quickly, leaving the Han people no time to organize and defend themselves.

3. The Hui people had a significant advantage in certain areas, and concentrated their forces, dozens or even hundreds of times larger, to sweep away Han people, wiping out entire villages and counties.

4. The Hui people had weapons and cavalry, while the Han people did not.

As is well known, Islam is a complete system of politics, economics, military affairs, culture, and daily life packaged under the guise of religion. It is a theocratic ideology, and the religious communities built around mosques are the basic organizations of Islam . These communities have strong mobilization and organizational capabilities. Muslim communities usually maintain contact through religious activities, but when the opportunity arises, they can quickly organize themselves through these communities. As Turkish President Erdogan said, " Mosques are our barracks, and believers are our soldiers. "

Islam’s mobilization mechanism is similar to that of nomadic militias, where people serve as civilians in peacetime and as soldiers in times of war. From Europe to Australia, rapid gatherings of Muslim communities are frequently observed. In the 2004 Zhongmu incident in Henan, thousands of Hui Muslims were mobilized from other counties and even provinces through religious organizations. One can imagine that if this had occurred in a context similar to the Tongzhi era, the unsuspecting Han Chinese in Zhongmu could have suffered enormous losses.

In some areas where religious forces are active, Islamic religious organizations have effectively superseded local governments, becoming the de facto managers of social life. In the Wangji Maqi Mosque in Dongxiang County, Gansu Province, the imam, believing television sets violated Islamic law, led his men to confiscate and burn television sets from the homes of believers. There have also been reports of Muslims in mosques in Henan Province enforcing Islamic law in the streets. In many inland provinces, it is no longer news that Muslim groups openly enter Han Chinese homes to confiscate alcohol. In 2009, Muslims in Shadian District, Yunnan Province, formed an anti-alcohol group to “ensure a ‘clean Ramadan’ in Shadian,” forcibly entering non-Muslim homes to search and confiscate food that violated Islamic law , forcing non-Muslims to comply with Islamic law.

Muslims openly inspect Han Chinese homes for alcohol.

In recent years, China has faced escalating threats from Islamic extremists, with serious terrorist attacks occurring in places like Yili and Kunming. This is closely related to the increasing prevalence of halalization in the northwest and southwest regions. The most typical manifestation of this is the massive expansion of mosque construction, which has made Xinjiang the world’s largest city for mosques, far surpassing religious countries like Saudi Arabia and Iran. More than 70% of these mosques were built in the last 30 years. In the early 1990s, Xinjiang had only over 9,000 mosques; in just 30 years, this number has grown to over 24,000 (Note: Iran has 60 million Muslims and 5,400 mosques nationwide) . With 2,371 administrative villages, this translates to an average of 10 mosques per village and one mosque for every 600 people. The number of mosques far exceeds the number of schools. If the money spent on mosque construction were used to build primary and secondary schools, Xinjiang’s education level would significantly improve, making young people less susceptible to indoctrination and attraction by extremism. Massacre cause

III. Why do Hui Muslims massacre Han Chinese?

From the perspective of Islamic doctrine, it is easy to explain that the Hui Muslim army’s banner was " Overthrow the Qing and destroy the Han " and " Kill all the Kafir “.

Although there was no unified command, the various Hui Muslim armies all adhered to one principle: start in the countryside, indiscriminately killing Han Chinese regardless of gender or age. This was not for financial gain or land acquisition; after wiping out the Han Chinese in the countryside, they would then massacre county towns and besiege the provincial capital. Their goal was very clear: to exterminate all Han Chinese in Shaanxi and Gansu provinces, eradicate all Han culture in the region, and subsequently establish a purely Islamic state.

Under this brutal policy of genocide, there were over a hundred instances of massacres involving more than 100,000 people at a time. The Han Chinese in over a hundred counties in Shaanxi and Gansu provinces were almost completely wiped out, and it took a decade for some counties to be repopulated and rebuild. The cruelty and brutality of Hui Muslims far exceeded that of ISIS today, not to mention the Nazi genocide of Jews during wartime and the Nanjing Massacre perpetrated by the Japanese army.

This is a rare religious massacre in Chinese history.

Hui minority?

IV. Are Hui people a minority?

There has never been an ethnic group in Chinese history called “Hui”.

After Islam was introduced to mainland China, it was Sinicized and became known as Hui. Aside from some Arabs, Persians, and other Central Asians from the Western Regions, most Hui believers were Han Chinese. During the Ming Dynasty, there was only the religion of Hui, without the concept of a Hui ethnic group.

During the Qianlong era of the Qing Dynasty , in order to weaken the dominant Han Chinese, a national policy of Manchu, Mongol, Tibetan, Hui, and Han was established, formally classifying the Hui community as a separate ethnic group . Over two centuries of evolution, the Hui people’s sense of national independence has grown stronger, and they have become a completely independent ethnic group, with Islam becoming thoroughly Islamized.

According to the Chinese tradition of tracing lineage by blood, Hui people are Han Chinese who practice Islam . However, when interacting with Hui Muslims, they often claim to be of Arab descent, believing themselves to be superior to Han Chinese in both lineage and language. If a Han Chinese converts to Islam, they are called “Han Mu,” and in the eyes of Hui Muslims, Han Mu are certainly not as good as orthodox Muslims.

Assimilability

V. Is the Han Chinese people highly capable of assimilation?

It’s not as powerful as imagined. In fact, judging from the number of Hui Muslims in China, a large number of Han Chinese have been assimilated by Islam.

For Muslims, “There is only God. There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the messenger of God.” Religious identity far surpasses national and kinship ties; all Muslims are one family. Islamic ideology spreads in the name of religion, exhibiting both considerable closedness and expansionism. Let’s take population as an example to see how the Muslim population has expanded.

Islam is internally closed: the offspring of an Islamic family must be Muslim, and apostates are severely punished. Even within the Islamic system, it is difficult to survive independently after leaving the faith. Islam encourages procreation, and the “uterine weapon” has swept the world.

The Muslim population is expanding outwards: in Islam-dominated areas, non-believers are discriminated against and severely exploited, and over time, people are forced to convert to Islam; if Han Chinese intermarry with Muslims, according to their doctrines, the Han Chinese must convert to Islam and live according to their lifestyle and religious beliefs.

Islam, with its policy of only accepting immigrants and never releasing them, thus achieved continuous population expansion, which in turn meant continuous territorial expansion. Whoever could concentrate a larger population held the advantage in the age of cold weapons. This was the secret to Islam’s continuous expansion for over a thousand years before industrialization. Before the Tongzhi Hui Rebellion, the number of Muslims increased dramatically from southwest to northwest, with the Hui population in Shaanxi and Gansu alone reaching over eight million.

If you view Islam merely as a religion, you can only marvel at its power of control. In reality, the core of Islam’s assimilation ability lies not in the influence of religion itself, but in its comprehensive control over the Muslim community, hidden behind the religious mask, through various means including politics, law, economics, daily life, customs, language, and rituals.

Islamism’s closed nature, controlling power, and corrosiveness are unparalleled by any other religion or ideology in the world, and Chinese civilization is equally powerless against it.

For example, regarding language control, Hui Muslims take pride in being able to read and speak Arabic because the Quran is written in Arabic. There are many recent examples as well. After the outbreak of the Iraq War, Hui Muslims in many parts of Gansu and Yunnan provinces took to the streets to protest and riot, strongly supporting Saddam Hussein. For a period, Ningxia TV’s daily weather forecast included weather forecasts for Islamic countries in the Middle East and North Africa, but this was discontinued after strong public criticism.

Zuo Zongtang

VI. Historical Evaluation of Zuo Zongtang

If Zuo Zongtang hadn’t intervened and led the Hunan Army into Shaanxi, the Han Chinese in Shaanxi and Gansu provinces might very well have been wiped out. If the Hui Muslim army had been given space to live and govern themselves, the Shaanxi-Gansu region would have become Islamized, and the entire western region, including Xinjiang, would have quickly separated.

After suppressing the Shaanxi-Gansu rebellion, Zuo Zongtang, defying the incompetent advice of high-ranking officials like Li Hongzhang, insisted on the military reconquest of Xinjiang. Undeterred by the arduous journey, Zuo led his army westward, reclaiming Xinjiang from the Turkish bandit Yaqub Beg, who had been invading and dividing China, while simultaneously thwarting the Russian Tsar’s attempt to invade China.

For over a thousand years, from the Sui and Tang dynasties and even earlier, the Islamic Turks have coveted Xinjiang, waiting for an opportunity to strike. The recent East Turkistan is simply the latest force of Yaqub Beg. Zuo Zongtang, a native of Xiangyin, Hunan, overcame countless difficulties with great courage, established himself in Gansu and Shaanxi, and recovered Xinjiang. His extraordinary achievements in safeguarding the unity of the motherland and defending the borders ensured more than a century of peace in Northwest China. His remarkable accomplishments are comparable to those of Zhang Qian and Ban Chao!

Zuo Zongtang was a great man!

summary

The Tongzhi Hui Rebellion was not an ordinary internal conflict, but a religious war—a war waged by Muslims against non-Muslims. More precisely, it was a war of genocide waged by Hui Muslims against Han Chinese non-Muslims.

Learning from past mistakes is crucial for future success. For continental countries, generally, the stronger the dominant ethnic group, the less likely the country is to experience turmoil and division. Conversely, if ethnic groups are evenly matched in certain regions, and Islamism flourishes, unrest is more likely. It’s important to understand that even if Kim Jong-un of North Korea is acting with impunity, he’s only concerned with his own territory. Islam is different; no region bordering a Muslim community in the world is truly at peace. The divisions of Yugoslavia and Sudan serve as stark examples, and the current troubles faced by Chechnya in Russia, southern Thailand, and northern Myanmar should serve as a warning to China. Chechnya is geographically remote, so its loss wouldn’t significantly impact Russia. However, if China loses its northwest, the cradle of Chinese civilization, its entire inland region would lack defensive depth, leaving it directly threatened by external powers, and the demise of Chinese civilization would be imminent.

Unlike island nations, continental countries face immense survival pressures. If the Chinese people are brainwashed by the beautiful slogans of " universal harmony, equality and fraternity ,” becoming completely immersed in national and ethnic nihilism while ignoring the survival space of the nation and its people, then the Chinese people will have nowhere to be buried.

In March of this year, Xinjiang promulgated the “Regulations on De-extremification of the Autonomous Region,” indicating that the top leadership has recognized the severity of the problem. This is a good start, but it is far from enough; addressing the symptoms is not enough; we must address the root causes. Here are a few points to consider:

1. Upgrade the “De-radicalization Regulations” . De-radicalization is a problem faced by the whole country, and the regulations should be upgraded to a basic national law and implemented nationwide as soon as possible.

2. Abolish the family planning policy . The family planning policy targeting the Han Chinese is tantamount to self-destruction. Population is the foundation of a nation. The Han Chinese have narrowly escaped extermination by nomadic tribes several times in history, thanks to their large population and vast territory.

3. Eliminate the privileges of “fewer privileges and more lenient treatment .” Before the law, there should be no distinction based on ethnicity or religion. Granting privileges to a minority is the root cause of social unrest.

4. Abolish the ethnic autonomous regions and restore the province system . The practice of establishing ethnic autonomous regions has numerous hidden dangers. First, it grants privileges to certain ethnic groups, infringing upon the interests of other ethnic groups and violating the constitutional principle of equality. Second, in practice, it exacerbates ethnic conflicts and is detrimental to social stability. Third, it provides potential support for separatist forces.

5. Pay attention to the problem of undermining local government power. The situation where religious power overrides local government power in some areas should be given sufficient attention; otherwise, if it spreads, the consequences could be disastrous.

6. Practices in accordance with Sharia law are strictly prohibited . This includes occupying public prayer spaces, granting privileges regarding halal food, and mandating face coverings. A small number of local governments and public officials have assisted in promoting Sharia law; these individuals should be dealt with seriously.

Europe has embarked on a path of no return; how can China remain unaffected? We must remain vigilant at all times!
