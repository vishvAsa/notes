The Zhōulǐ is a complete slog.
45,000 characters of irrelevant, outdated bureaucratic drivel.
Did people really have to memorize the number of clerks, attendants, etc. verbatim for the Imperial Examination? 
No wonder it was replaced by the Eight-Legged Essay and the curriculum had shifted heavily toward the Four Books instead.
By the time of the Míng dynasty they had stopped using dagger axes, bronze swords, and chariots in warfare, cowrie shells as currency, jǐngtián (well-field) system in the communal farming, and tortoise shells and cattle bones in divination for millennia.
It's as if the IRS's standard operating procedures became an "American Classic" two hundred years from now or asking a modern MBA student to memorize the mechanical specs of a 1980s VCR or, more accurately, the logistics of the Pony Express route.
