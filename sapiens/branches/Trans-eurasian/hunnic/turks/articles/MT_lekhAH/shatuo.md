Shatuo Turks

## Alleigtences
The Shatuo Turks appear to have emerged as part of the Western Türkic confederation after the split of the First Turkic Khaghanate. Their name, Shatuo, meaning "sandy or desert slopes," suggests that they emerged in the desert regions of what is today Xinjiang, near the eastern Tianshan Mountains. 

During the expansion of the Tibetan Empire, the Shatuo were subjugated and made to serve as a vanguard for the Tibetans. However, they switched allegiance to the Tang Dynasty and migrated to settle in the Ordos region of Inner Mongolia. The Tang utilized them as a buffer force against their steppe cousins.

## Huang Chao Rebellion
During the devastating Huang Chao Rebellion (875–884 CE), which nearly destroyed the Tang dynasty, the Shatuo leader Li Keyong, famously known as "One-Eyed Dragon",  came to the aid of the Chinese imperial dynasty. He swept across the northern corridor with his so-called "Black-Crow Army" (due to their black uniforms) and recaptured the capital, Chang'an, for the Tang in 883 CE. 

## 3 of 5 dynasties
Nevertheless, the Tang collapsed in 907 CE, and their erstwhile protectors, the Shatuo Turks, seized power to found three dynasties during the "Five Dynasties" period  to rule northern China in rapid succession:

1. Later Tang (923–936 CE), founded by Li Keyong's son Li Cunxu.
2. Later Jin (936–947 CE), founded by Shi Jingtang. They formed an alliance with the Khitans, descendants of the Para-Mongol Xianbei Khaghanate, who then took Beijing.
3. Later Han (947–951 CE), founded by Liu Zhiyuan.

## Li Keyong - hybrid
Chinese researchers have extracted the DNA and sequenced the genome of Li Keyong, who was buried in a stone sarcophagus in Shanxi. Source: [TW](https://sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1673852726001475) The interesting findings are:

1\. He is a nearly half-and-half hybrid, 53.4% Ancient Northeast Asian (ANA) and 46.6% Western Eurasian Steppe. This adds to the evidence that the Turks and the Mongols emerged through the absorption of Indo-Iranians who had founded earlier steppe potentates like the shaka-s, kuShANa-s, ashvin-s (<Wusun), arimaspa-s, sairima-s (Sarmatians) and the like. 

The process of the merger of Indo-Europeans and Eastern groups had begun early, right in the dramatic and mysterious Okunevo culture. However, the events leading to the ethnogenesis of the Shatuo elite were later mergers, specifically from the Sintashta-derived groups and more Eastern groups of Mongolia.

2\. His Y-haplogroup R1a1a1b2∼AM01870 is part of the lineage of R1a that was brought by the Indo-Aryan to India. This clinches the fact that his male line was of the Indo-Iranian ancestry. 

This, in turn, suggests that the Shatuo Turks emerged through the Turkification of an earlier Indo-Iranian group in Xinjiang during the rise of the first Türk Khaghanate.

3\. In the PCA analysis, he falls within a clear cluster (enclosed in red) that includes Turks, Mongols, Khitans and Tungus. 

This is in keeping with the linguistic **_convergence_**, or Sprachbund formation, among these originally distinct groups that mixed together to become genetically related.
