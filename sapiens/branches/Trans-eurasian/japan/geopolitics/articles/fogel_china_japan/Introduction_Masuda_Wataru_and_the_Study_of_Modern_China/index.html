Masuda Wataru, the author of the text that follows was born in a small fishing village in Shimane prefecture in 1903, the only son of a medical doctor.1

Excelling at school, he entered Tokyo Imperial University‚Äôs Department of Chinese Literature in 1926. It was not a particularly happy time to study there in the immediate aftermath of the Great Kant≈ç Earthquake, and Masuda frequently made use of the Ueno Library at the time. The Faculty of Letters had been completely destroyed, and lectures were often held in makeshift bar-racks or in the operating rooms of the Medical School. He especially recalled enjoying the lectures of Shionoya On on the history of Chinese fiction and Taki Seiichi on East Asian art. He also studied with the great poet Sat≈ç Haruo and helped with his translations of Chinese fiction.

Upon graduation in 1929, he continued his translation work with Sat≈ç. Then, in March of 1931, he decided to go to Shanghai where good fortune enabled him to work and study under the great Chinese writer Lu Xun.
