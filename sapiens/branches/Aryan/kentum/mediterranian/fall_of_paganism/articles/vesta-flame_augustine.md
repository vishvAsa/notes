Christian Roman emperors such as Theodosius I famously closed pagan temples & extinguished the eternal flame of the Temple of Vesta to symbolize the death of the old Roman order & the transition from the "earthly city" to the "heavenly city."
The Visigoths under Alaric I brutally sacked Rome only 15 or 16 years later.
The pagans claimed that this Sack of Rome was due to the impiety of the Romans, which caused the Gods to withdraw their protection and permit the barbarians to breach the walls.

In response, Saint Augustine wrote his magnum opus in which he argued that the sack of Rome was ultimately inconsequential as all cities are temporary & imperfect while the true "Jerusalem," i.e. the heavenly Kingdom of God, could never be conquered.
