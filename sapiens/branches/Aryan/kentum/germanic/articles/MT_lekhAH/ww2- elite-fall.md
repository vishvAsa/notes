## WW2 fall
Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2036647729707766234)

Estimates suggest that in WW1, the overall death rate for the British Army was approximately 12%. However, for officers drawn from elite households, it was closer to 18%. Subsequently, they lost a lot of their holdings to sell-offs, lost most of their domestic staff (more industrial jobs from the war), and many houses simply ended due to the absence of heirs. We suspect that in this lay the seeds of the end of the empire.+++(5)+++ 

To their credit, the English elite often led from the front like the rAjpUt counterparts and took heavy losses through the war as they engaged in brutal trench warfare with their German cousins. The ferocity of this is evocatively recorded by the remarkable German entomologist-warrior Ernst Jünger. 

A similar thing happened on the German side: Approximately 14% of the German enlisted men were killed in WW1. 

However, the death rate for the German elite (especially the Prussian warriors) is given as 24%. While the Germans did not have much of an empire, these losses to the high human capital seem to have had effects to this day.


## Science importance
Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2036661081280507913)

Below is a small list of illustrious English figures that came to my mind at the instance. They are divided into high-level and mid-level elite, as per my understanding, and commoners. While some commoners had upward mobility, the high and mid-level elite give a picture of the human capital in that bracket. One can extrapolate from that what their partial decimation could do to a country. A parallel can be seen in the decimation of the v1 Hindu elite by the marUnamatta-s in North India.

### High level
- Robert Boyle: Gas law, "father of English chemistry."
- Henry Cavendish: gravitation/discovery og hydrogen.
- William Parsons: built the largest telescope in mid-1800s, theory of galaxies
- Lord Rayleigh: noble gas discovery (e.g., Argon), Rayleigh scattering.
- Lord Kelvin: thermodynamics and the transatlantic cable.

### Mid-rung elite
- John Goodricke and Edward Pigott: discovery and theory of variable stars.
- Charles Darwin: Evolutionary theory.
- Humphrey Davy: electrochemistry.
- William Jones: Indo-European theory.

### Commoners
- Issac Newton: foundations of physics, calculus, conics/cubics.
- Michael Faraday: Electromagnetism, electrochemistry.
- Wilhelm Herschel was born a German commoner, but his son John Herschel ascended to the English elite.
