विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

Shri Gajanan Bhaskar Mehendale, Renowned Historian and Biographer of Shivaji MaharajPlace of lecture : Shri Sadguru Jangli Maharaj Samadhi Mandir, PuneDated : Ashadh Vadya 9, Shak 1937, Shivashak 342 (10th July 2015)Translation errors can be attributed to Rohit Sahasrabudhe

Immersion in shivAjI

I first stepped into Bharat Itihaas Samshodhak Mandal in 1969, to study the biography of Shivaji Maharaj. I was 22 years of age at that time, now my age is 68. In this long period, hardly a couple of days may have gone by when I did not read anything about Shivaji Maharaj’s life.

During this period, I penned Shivaji Maharaj’s biography in about two volumes with 2500 pages. In those 2500 pages, the references themselves total up to 7000. Now I am planning to write third volume and the fourth and last in the next year. I have also written a biography on Shivaji Maharaj in English. It is complete and has around thousand pages. So total of 2500 pages in Marathi and 1000 pages in English are completed, and more will follow

. I have not done this as a sacrifice. Living with Shivaji Maharaj is a joy and there is nothing sacrificial about it. And I am practically living with this ’larger than life’ figure in the form of documents for past 45 years, so I have come to know many things about him. The important thing that I am going to speak is about the misinformation that is spreading about him, either deliberately or due to lack of knowledge.

Masjid building lies

For example, someone was asking and confirming with me if Shivaji Maharaj built a Masjid on Raigad. I have already explained how long I have been studying Shivaji Maharaj’s biography, and I will tell the truth and the whole truth. Shivaji Maharaj has nowhere and never built a Masjid.+++(5)+++ Never means never ever. If someone tells you that he built a Masjid then send that person to me.

No new grants to masjids

I would like to share another important thing, many people believe that Shivaji Maharaj gave grants to Masjids. If he had given those grants, I would have said so because it’s not an expense to my pocket, it’s something that he had expended on, if at all it was done. Now someone may want to think that it should have been given but it is their wish. So the point is whether Shivaji Maharaj gave it or not. Lets leave aside what you think about it or what I think about it. There is not a shred of evidence which shows that Shivaji Maharaj gave a new grant or award to any Masjid. If anyone tells you otherwise, it is a lie. I have read all (available) references related to Shivaji Maharaj’s biography. All including Marathi, Pharsi, Portuguese and he has never given a new grant to any Masjid. Please listen carefully to what I am saying, no NEW grant was given.

New muslim grants to temples?

As my Guru Prof.G.H.Khare, a renowned person, would say, and (also) what I have come to know from the study of documents, that a new grant given by a Muslim king to a Hindu temple is very rarely seen. There might be a one-of example, if at all. For example, there was a ruler called Ibrahim Adilshah. He was almost following Hindu religion. He had composed hymns on Lord Ganesh. Despite being a Hindu by birth, I have never composed a hymn on Lord Ganesh but this Ibrahim Adilshah had done that. How can he then remain a muslim ? That practically makes him a Hindu, by practice he was a Hindu. So in such exceptional cases a (muslim) king has given a grant to a temple. But normally, that’s not the case.

Old grants continued

Then how do we justify the ongoing grants ? These kings had Hindu sardars (military officers) working for him. He also had Hindu (administrative) officers. When someone is working for us, we cannot hurt them. If we want them to work diligently then we have to take care of them. These Hindus would internally continue these grants which is why they continued under Muslim rule. Two three Masjids also had received grant continuance under Shivaji Maharaj’s rule.+++(4)+++ Meaning existing grant continued, not a new grant, an exception to that is the Masjid at Indapur.

Exception

The Kazi of this Masjid has said, and it’s available in the documents, I am just paraphrasing him, that our grant was not continued under Shivaji Maharaj’s rule. Shivacharitra Sahitya Khand 3, a documented volume has this detail, which you can verify. Now this Kazi, who is a judge, he won’t lie. He says and I am just quoting him that our grant was discontinued under Shivaji Maharaj’s rule. So if some ’learned’ person tells you that it was continued, then it’s a lie. It was not continued. Two or three grants were continued, but thats all. No new grants were given.

Objective

Now what was the objective behind Shivaji Maharaj’s kingdom ? I have written this in detail in my English book, it will be published in the Marathi book soon, hence mentioning it.

This (subject) has two or three aspects.

shivAjI on turks

One is what did Shivaji Maharaj himself say and do. If we have to quote Shivaji Maharaj, then in a letter to his brother Vyankoji, he says that I kill Turks, the word Turk was used to denote Muslims in old documents (even today, Marathi spoken near Thanjavur uses the word Turuk for Muslims), so he says that

‘I kill Turks and you have Turks in your ranks, then how will you succeed ? It is impossible.’ +++(4)+++

Saptakoteshwar temple

There is a Saptakoteshwar temple in Goa in Narve village. It was destroyed by Portuguese, before that by Adilshah and many others. Shivaji Maharaj reconstructed the temple. Why did he do that ? Think about it. Actually, he had ample space to build temples in Pune. Then why did he build it over there ? The answer is, the way you insisted on demolishing it, I insist on rebuilding it.+++(5)+++ That is why it was rebuilt. There are many such examples in his life.

Shonachalapati temple

I will share one more example. It was Karnataka then, now it’s in Tamilnadu. There is a Shonachalapati temple in Thiruvannamalai, it was demolished. Shivaji Maharaj insisted and had it rebuilt. You can say it was insistence, if you may, but he rebuilt it.

Bhivandi masjids

There was a English traveler called Fryer, a doctor in that time, studied in college to become a doctor. They had such colleges in that time too. This Fryer visited Bhivandi. He says that Shivaji would have demolished the Masjids over there but instead used them as storehouses. This is what Fryer says.

There was an abandoned Masjid. The Havaldar of that region, meaning Mamledar of todays world told him to go and stay there, so he was staying there. Fryer himself tells this.

विस्तारः (द्रष्टुं नोद्यम्)

काया देThe remaining Buildings having many Stories of square facing Stones, and the Mosques, which are numerous, of the same, abating little of their ancient Lustre, being all watered with delicate Tanks; about which are costly Tombs with their distinct Chappels or Mosques, where formerly the Mullahs² had fat Pensions to pray for the departed Souls, which is maintained by them as efficacious; wherefore they covet Funerals in the most conspicuous Places, which the Pleasant Summer houses hanging over here, cause these Places to be;

the unemploy’d People of the Town daily wasting their time in these inviting Varieties;
which is the only thing pleads for their continuance,

Seva Gi as a Gentu being otherwise inclinable to raze them; yet purposely to pervert them from the use of the Donors, and Intention of the Founders, orders them to be converted into Granaries, especially those within the City.

Translation:

Modern English Translation:

The remaining buildings, constructed with many stories of square-cut facing stones, along with the numerous mosques of the same material, have lost little of their ancient splendor. They are all equipped with beautiful water tanks, around which stand costly tombs with their own separate chapels or mosques.

In these places, the Mullahs formerly received generous pensions to pray for the souls of the departed—a practice they maintain as effective to this day. This is why they eagerly seek funerals in the most prominent locations. The pleasant summer houses overlooking these areas make the spots even more attractive, causing the unemployed townspeople to waste their days in these inviting places. This is the only reason they are allowed to continue.

Shivaji (Seva Gi), being a Gentu (Hindu), would otherwise have been inclined to demolish them. Yet, deliberately to misuse them contrary to the donors’ and founders’ original purpose, he ordered them to be converted into granaries—especially those inside the city.

Masjid desecration

There are Jesuit Padres in Tamilnadu. There is a Christian sect called Jesuit, they obstinate and are orthodox, spend their whole life in converting people, they don’t succeed much but they try. These Jesuits have written that Shivaji Maharaj desecrated Masjids over here.

This is not something that I am telling of my own accord, I am sharing what the Jesuit Padres have said. After all, they are Godly men, they would not lie. This is about Shivaji Maharaj … words.

Islam stunted

I have already shared the words of Shivaji Maharaj in his letter to Vyankoji Maharaj and now his actions too. Now I will explain what his enemies have said.

There is a farmaan (order) from Ali Adilshah to Kanhoji Jedhe. I have seen and read the original order, it’s in Farsi, I can read Farsi. It says that due to Shivaji Maharaj, well it actually just says “Shivaji” because they usually begin with abuses. So they don’t mention ‘Shivaji Maharaj’ but we should say that. It says that the growth of Muslim religion is stunted due to Shivaji Maharaj. This is what they say. And that is why I have sent Afzalkhan against him is what it says in Ali Adilshah’s farman.

mlechCha-xaya-dIxita

I have shared what his enemies have said, and I will give more examples. Now let’s see what his friends have said, friends meaning people on his side. There is a grant letter from Sambhaji Maharaj. It’s in Sanskrit. He knew Sanskrit, the letter contains a verse in his handwriting.

Where is this grant letter ? If anyone wants to see it, it is in the Shikshan Prasarak Mandali office behind S.P.College on Tilak road. It’s photo has been published, of the complete letter, it’s in Sadhan Chikitsa written by V.S.Bendre.

In this grant letter, he (Sambhaji Maharaj) describes his ancestors, it was a practice to do so, to eulogize their deeds. It says that, ‘My father, who was he ?’ … (he was) MlechhakshayadIkshita !!!

It means that he had, from a young age, taken upon him (the job to) to destroy Mlechhas (muslims). Who says this ? Sambhaji Maharaj is saying this ! A son would normally know (about) his father well. But nowadays people are telling something very different whereas Sambhaji Maharaj has said something else. We should have more trust on what Sambhaji Maharaj has said.

Under hindu rule

And it’s not just from Sambhaji Maharaj, his officer located at a village called Hadkolan near Goa. There is an inscription which says that villagers requested him to abolish taxes, which he did and it is mentioned in this inscription. What does it say ? It is also published in the biography of Sambhaji Maharaj written by Kamal Gokhale, you can see that. It says that ‘Now this is under Hindu rule’ ! This is a sentence from the request made by the villagers. It’s not my sentence.

Henry Revington

So, in short, I have told what Shivaji Maharaj himself said and also what he actually did. I also shared what his enemies, like Ali Adilshah, and friends have said. Now let’s see what few comparatively neutral people have said. Many would have heard about Henry Revington, he had set off cannons on Shivaji Maharaj at Panhalgad. He has written a letter to Shivaji Maharaj, it is published in English Records on Shivaji. The letter itself begins with addressing him as ‘General of Hindu forces’ ! Who is saying this ? An Englishman. He was a shrewd man, he had judged Shivaji Maharaj very well. He was the first person to dream of eventually establishing British rule in India, he was such a visionary and he had understood Shivaji Maharaj very well. He has addressed Shivaji Maharaj as ‘General of Hindu forces’ !

Insulting muslims

So Shivaji Maharaj’s words, his deeds, the words and deeds of his enemies, his contemporaries, like Sambhaji Maharaj, Kavi Bhushan, … his biographer Kavi Parmanand, who had seen him up close, says that he (Shivaji Maharaj) has been insulting muslims from a young age. This is mentioned in Shivabharat !

So be it someone like Parmanand, who saw him up close, be it Kavi Bhushan, … what Guruji said sometime back, the author of verses ‘Sunnat hoti sabki’, he had seen Shivaji Maharaj in person, he went to Raigad, he was from the Hindi heartland.

Tryambakeshvar temple

So all these contemporary people and their later traditions also have the same understanding about Maratha kingdom. I will explain by giving an example. The temple at Tryambakeshvar is built after demolishing a Masjid. My friend Pandurang Balkavade is probably not here today, he has the entire details about that temple from the beginning, right from setting the foundation stone. This Trimbakeshwar temple was constructed after demolishing a Masjid.

Sundarnarayana temple

I will tell about another such temple, it’s from Nashik, if anyone is from Nashik, there is a Sundarnarayan temple over there, it is also built after demolishing an Islamic structure. There are records which establish this. They are published in Shivacharitra Sahitya Vol.2.

Objective

So what was the objective of Shivaji Maharaj’s kingdom ? Somebody might say that he should not have done this and should have respected our secularism. It is possible that someone may think that way, but for now, let’s see what Shivaji Maharaj thought.

What people think, what I think, I think it would have been better if he had treated everyone equally but he did not think that way, he will behave as per his wish, not as per yours or mine or someone else’, will he ? Therefore, this is what he did.

Muslim despot names

Today when I look around me, … in 1971-72 I had the misfortune of visiting Delhi. And there I saw a big road called Aurangzeb road. There is a Lodi road, who was he ? It is a huge road. I said, does anyone think about naming something after Shivaji Maharaj ? I had just started studying two years back. So they said, this is a small lane named after Shivaji Maharaj. That’s nice, I said (sarcastically). There is a Marathi settlement in Karol Bagh, I expressed my anguish about the Aurangzeb road in Delhi to a Marathi person from there. He said back in your state Maharashtra, you have an entire city named after him. Shame on you ! It was a slap on my face.

Fortunately, I never had to visit this city because even though peeople today call it Sambhajinagar, the official name still is Aurangabad. And this is a sin. To honour someone who killed Sambhaji Maharaj after torturing him for days, I don’t think there is an equivalent of such shamelessness in any society. Nowhere in the world. At least I don’t know of any such example.

So I had thought that eventually the government would think about this change, when I went to Delhi I had thought that there would be a huge road named after Shivaji Maharaj, another named after Harihar and Bukka, Raja Pratap, Ranjit Singh, and these are huge regal figures, that roads would be named after Dnyaneshwar, Tukaram, Tulsidas, Narsi Mehta but all these are on the sidelines and we have Lodi road, Aurangzeb road. Are we in the capital of India or in Lahore (Pakistan) ?

Muslim employment till 1657

Now some people claim that Shivaji Maharaj employed many muslims. The other day a ’learned’ man said that there were one lakh muslims in Shivaji Maharaj’s army. I came to know that he said that, I was not present there, … why should I go to such places, but I heard from others. So I am sharing this for your benefit, about who all were in Shivaji Maharaj’s army, what was their strength, I will share everything. There were 4 or 5 muslims in his service till 1657.+++(4)+++

There is an Adilshahi farman dated 1658 to Shahji Raja saying that he will not be held responsible for his son’s actions. Now what is this farman about ? From 1658, Shivaji Maharaj openly declared his region as an independent kingdom, meaning his first direct assault on Adilshahi is in 1658. Before that, he was operating here as his father’s representative. He had a big jahgir of 1200 villages. It was small compared to what Shahji Raja had in Karnataka though. So he was managing these 1200 villages.

Admin officers

I will tell you about the officers working here at that time, till 10th March 1657. There was one called Siddi Amber Baghdadi, he may be from Baghdad. You may have heard, the place near Baghdad, where nowadays people are beheaded, its their tradition. So this Baghdadi, … he was the Havaldar of Pune.

Jaina Khan Pirzade was the Sarhavaldar, meaning Collector in today’s terms.

Behelim Khan was the Havaldar of Baramati.

These three names which I have mentioned are the only three. After 1657, not a shred of them is seen anywhere. They just vanished.

I have already said that, Shivaji Maharaj officially started his own kingdom in 1658. After 1657, not a single muslim administrative officer, I am repeating, not even one, is found. Apart from the three mentioned till 10th March 1657, nobody else is found, because there are none.

Noorkhan Beg

Many people have tall claims, about someone called Noorkhan Beg, who was a Sarnaubat (General). He is last seen in the document dated 10 March 1657. After that, Noorkhan Beg is gone, he just vanishes. What happened of him is not known to us. The person who succeeded him as infantry General was Yesaji Kank.

Siddi Hilal

I have mentioned four people, now I will tell about the fifth. He was Siddi Hilal. He was with and against Shivaji Maharaj, sometime also with Adilshahi, but where was he from ? He was the Kritaputra, meaning a slave but brought up like a son, of Kheloji Bhonsale.

one might think, why was he not converted to Hinduism ? It would not be wrong to think so. But the problem was that in that time, if someone was not a Hindu by birth, there was no provision to get him converted. Till the 19th century there is no example of a person who is not born as Hindu and has been converted to Hinduism.

Much earlier, the Shak, Kushan, Hun had such examples but how did they convert ? It was through generations of acceptance of local culture and traditions. Suddenly some muslim or christian converting to Hinduism was not possible. The reason behind that was which caste would you associate to him ? No caste would be ready to accept a foreigner. So even if someone was ready to be converted, it was not possible.

The case for Netoji Palkar was different. He was a Hindu by birth and was forcefully converted, so he could be reconverted. Someone who was not a Hindu by birth or whose forefathers were not Hindu, could not be converted. Otherwise, Siddi Hilal would surely have been converted. So that is about Siddi Hilal.

Admirals

Now about Shivaji Maharaj’s two admirals, who were muslims. One was Daulatkhan and other Daryasarang.

Shivaji Maharaj himself (ordered) arrested Daryasarang in 1679 and his story ends there.

Now this Daulatkhan, why was he appointed ? You can check this on internet, when India got independence in 1947, our navy and air-force was headed by a British officer for 10 years. Not Indian but British, till 10 years after independence - why ? Because we did not have that expertise to handle that charge. Britishers never gave such high ranks to Indian officers. It was the same situation when Shivaji Maharaj started his naval activity. We did not have expertise in naval warfare, so Daulatkhan was appointed under his control. Later Angre and Dhulup took over, but after this not a single muslim officer is seen. Not once. It was only till our men got the required expertise.

Madari Mehtar

So the names which I have just told you, a name or two may have slipped off … now about Madari Mehtar. It is fake, simply untrue. There is not a shred of truth in it. The incidence has been penned after 1800 AD and what does it say ? That Sawai Jaisingh campaigned against Shivaji Maharaj. Now this Sawai Jaisingh is the great great grandson of Mirzaraje Jaisingh. Shivaji Maharaj was not even alive at that time. So how can we believe the actions of Madari Mehtar during Sawai Jaisingh’s campaign ? There is no truth in there. Anyone will claim such things, that my ancestors were contemporaries of Shivaji Maharaj, even I will claim, but it needs substantiated proof, documents. So this document is of a much later period.

Conclusion

In all, other than the 8-10 names given above, there was no muslim officer in Shivaji Maharaj’s army. That is one.Secondly, there were no muslim administrative officers in his kingdom. Zero. None.

And in the army, I have said that Siddi Hilal or Daulatkhan or Daryasarang or their Farsi clerk named Kazi Hyder, and he did the right thing by joining Aurangzeb during Sambhaji Maharaj’s rule in 1682, meaning he chose the right place. So how can I say that this Kazi Hyder was loyal ?

Therefore, some people say some absurd things … now if Shivaji Maharaj had employed any muslims, he was going to pay their salary, it was not an expense on my pocket. So there is no reason why I would hide it, but it is the job of a historian to tell the truth, since people nowadays are spreading lies, reluctantly I have to tell the truth.

Saptakoteshvar temple

I think, should I stop, Guruji (Sambhajirao Bhide Guruji) ? Shall I continue for 5 minutes, okay …

I said earlier that Shivaji Maharaj insisted on rebuilding the Saptakoteshwar temple … why did I say insist ? Why did he insist ? It is because they insisted on demolishing it.

Rajyavyavaharkoshs

Similarly, there are two other things in his (Shivaji Maharaj’s) life. One is Rajyavyavaharkosh. Large amount of Farsi words had crept into Marathi language … and why did this happen ? It was not through voluntary mixture of culture, but because of imposed foreign rule. To clean this up, he got the Rajyavyavaharkosh written. It also says that Marathi language was corrupted by the influx of Muslim language and Rajyavyavaharkosh is written to cleanse it. It is written in its preface. Now see the similarity in two things, they deliberately demolished a temple, he deliberately rebuilt it, words were deliberately pushed into a language, he deliberately removed them and third thing is about Netoji Palkar.

Netaji Palkar

Was it of any harm if he would have remained a muslim ? After all, all religions are the same, actually it is not the case, they are not at all the same, but this lie is propagated. People who have not really studied any religion say that. All religions are inherently different. And when I say Hindu, it means the definition given by Babasaheb Ambedkar in article 25 of Indian constitution, it includes traditional Hindus like us, Sikh, Jain and Buddhists. All of these are included in the definition of Hinduism by Babasaheb Ambedkar and that is what I always mean, no other meaning crosses my mind. This is the only meaning I know of.

So the third thing that I told you about Netoji Palkar, … there wasn’t any harm if he would have remained a muslim, all religions are same, some people can think it that way but Shivaji Maharaj did not.

You converted him by force, I will insist on getting him reconverted. So there are three things, …
you ruined our language, I will clean it,
you converted our man, I will reconvert him,
you desecrated our temples, I will rebuild it.

This in a way is a positive insistence that is seen in Shivaji Maharaj, that is why he was a king and we are commoners. Guruji I will stop now.
