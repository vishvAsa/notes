The earliest pallava inscription is in prAkR^ita from Manchikallu in Guntur District. The early pallava copper plates are from the andhra coast, south of the kR^iShnA river. We believe they were an Iranic group that had settled in the region as subjects of the andhra-s. Only later they expanded into the tamiL country and defeated the choLa-s.

It is simple parsimony. The andhra-s have multiple inscriptions which say: shaka-yavana-pallava-nisUdana. Here the pallava refers to Iranic kShathrapa-s of pahAlavan ancestry. They had already entered India to begin a long assimilation. Now when they resurface as pallava-s, for me the strongest evidence is connecting them to a group already mentioned in prior inscriptions under that name. 

Parallel to this, the Iranian Rustam cycle has a claim of the descendants of Rustam marrying Indian royalty and becoming kings in south India. 

Finally, v2s are permitted to cite as gotra for ritual purposes that of their purohita-s. This has happened several times in H history; hence, the brahminical gotra of the pallava-s.
