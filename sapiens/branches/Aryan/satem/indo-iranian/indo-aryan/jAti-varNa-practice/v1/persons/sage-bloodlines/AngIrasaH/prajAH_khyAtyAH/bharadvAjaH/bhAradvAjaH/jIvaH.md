## Early Life
He was born around **1513 CE** in Bengal. He was the son of Anupama (spiritual name Vallabha), the youngest brother of Rūpa and Sanātana. He was a mere infant when his uncles renounced the world and left home. His father also passed away when Jīva was still a young boy.

**Child Prodigy:** From his earliest days, he was a genius of unbelievable caliber. He is said to have mastered Sanskrit grammar, poetry, and rhetoric in an astonishingly short time. He was also deeply devoted to Krishna from childhood, carving his own Deities to worship.

## The Divine Command
Though he never met Caitanya Mahāprabhu, it is said that he received a command in a dream-vision from Caitanya and Nityānanda Prabhu to leave his home and join his uncles in Vṛndāvana.

## Scholarly Preparation 
Understanding the magnitude of the mission, the young Jīva did not go directly to Vṛndāvana. He knew he needed to master all philosophical systems to establish the supremacy of Gaudiya Vaishnava thought.

*   First, he traveled to **Navadvipa**, the great center of learning in Bengal, to study Nyāya (logic) and other schools of thought.
*   Then, he went to **Vārāṇasī (Benares)**, the seat of Vedic orthodoxy. There he studied Vedānta under the tutelage of **Madhusūdana Sarasvatī**, one of the most famous Advaita Vedānta scholars in all of Indian history.

## Initiation and Service
**Arrival in Vṛndāvana:** Having completed his exhaustive studies and become a master of all competing philosophies, he finally made his way to Vṛndāvana around **1535 CE**, when he was approximately **22 years old**.

He was joyfully received by his uncles, Rūpa and Sanātana. He took formal initiation (*dīkṣā*) from Rūpa Gosvāmī and became his devoted student. He served his uncles humbly, assisting them in proofreading their manuscripts and compiling indexes. He absorbed their teachings and quickly became their most trusted intellectual heir, destined to complete their work and lead the mission into the future.
