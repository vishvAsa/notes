## Death date, Periya thirumudi adaivu
Periya thirumudi adaivu was written by son of Mamuni's sishya. Now this text refers to people who lived in 1570s. So holding Mamuni to have lived till 1443 only sounds impossible. Periya thirumudi adaivu claims that he lived till 125 yrs of age while later texts hold that he lived for less than 80 yrs. I think 125 could have been an attempt to abridge the gap that was seen. MMs sishya's son lived till 1570 (since PTA refers to seventh ahobilam jeeyar who lived in 1570s). Which means MM should have lived at least till 1490/1500.

## TVM Pillai regency
But thiruvaimozhipillai is claimed to have lived in madurai before mamunis birth and served as minister to pandyas. This is impossible given that it was sultanate rule from 1323 to 1371. Otoh, vijayanagar regents ruled in place of pandyan minor princes in early 1400s and thiruvaimozhipillai story matches with that date only.

### Direct shiShya?
But MM is mentioned as sishya of Tiruvaimozhi Pillai who is claimed to be student of Pillai Lokacharya. Even presuming that he got only pancasamskaram from PL, TVM Pillai must have been born in 1315 at the very latest. If MM was born in 1410 or so, then he could not be sishya of TVM Pillai. 

Quite possibly TVM Pillai was not direct sishya of PL. TVM Pillai is said to have ruled madurai as pandya regent. Madurai was under sultanate rule when the story is said to have occurred. Rather madurai temple chronicles record that pandyan princes were minors around 1400. TVM pillai must have been minister and part of regency rule during this time only.


## Conclusion
The birth dates of Adivan-shatakopa swamy and Manavalamamuni both should be brought to 1400s from their birthday in 1370-80 to reconcile the inscriptional evidences we find during the time of each and later day pontiffs of the former. 

AVS n MM should be dated to 1400s only. It is unfortunate that we didnt maintain proper historical records.
