## काश्यप-कृष्णः
A Living Vedanta Stalwart:  Sri K.S. Varadacharya  (KSV)

Today I would like to also share a few thoughts about a person who has taught me a great deal about Vedanta and darsana or philosophy. His guidance these last few years has changed my thinking in unexpected ways and has given me a profoundly deeper appreciation for Sri Ramanuja's philosophy. His name is Kothimangalam S. Varadacharya, KSV for short (photo attached).

Incidentally, Dr. M.A Alwar swamy who is teaching Sribhashyam over google hangout is one of the prime students of K.S Varadachar. Dr. M.A. Alwar has learnt under several other great scholars : E. S Varachar, S.M. Varadachar, Dr. Chamu, N.T Srinivasa Iyengar etc. who are all luminaries in their areas. We should remember that we are extremely lucky to study under Dr. M.A Alwar.

Once, while Dr. S.M.S Chari, a well known scholar of Visistadvaita was teaching me Vedanta Sutras, he remarked, "When I myself have any doubts in Vedanta, I consult two people -- N. T. Srinivasa Iyengar and Kothimangalam Varadacharya (KSV)."   I had already studied under N.T Srinivasa Iyengar for a few years when I was a teenager and was very familiar with him.  I had heard of K.S. Varadacharya but did not know him personally. When I asked Dr. Chari more about Sri Varadacharya, he just replied, "He is amazingly brilliant."  Dr. Chari never praised anyone easily so I knew I had to meet this person.

I first met KSV properly in this context two years ago in India. My sessions with him were brief but electrifying. He had a profoundly comprehensive way of addressing the core problems of philosophy, and no subject or text was unfamiliar to him.  I knew immediately that I was in the presence of a unique personality.

I returned to the US and requested his permission to begin a program of study with him via phone. Some of my friends -- Mani Varadarajan, Balaji Srinivasan, Dr. Manohara, Srinivasan Iyengar, Krishnamachari maama and a few others -- joined these conference calls, which took place once or twice weekly.  These lectures / Q&A sessions sometimes lasted over two hours and continued for two years.  KSV asked us to begin by not taking anything for granted, to set apart our biases and preconceptions, and to understand that philosophy was an attempt at seeking Truth, not a game of choosing one personality or convention over another.  He asked us never to default to "sampradaya", tradition. He taught us to consider the complete context of every acharya's writing -- philosophical, historical, social, political. He told us to always focus on the core questions, and not to devolve into textual reading-between-the-lines and microanalysis.

After just a few sessions, it was readily apparent to all of us that KSV was transforming our understanding of Vedanta.  Questions that had bothered us for years began to be addressed directly in a way no one ever had done before.

It is impossible for me to express in writing the depth of KSV's thinking. His views are unbelievably profound and enlightening. One can understand this only by directly talking to him.  Since he is currently in Mysore (Karnataka), interested people can go and meet him. He is about 91 years old now and is continuing his philosophical quest.  He is currently writing a book entitled "Satya Siddhi".

Given below is a translation of relevant portions of the article  "A Short introduction to the life of KSV"  written by:  Nadadur Sribhasya Simhasanam, Kalaimamani, S. N. Srirama DesikAcharya

This article was written as a preface to the book SarvankasA, a new commentary, authored by K.S Varadachar on Tattva MuktA kalApa of Sri Vedanta Desika.

I would like to add some information which we heard from KSV during our phone conversations with him:

"According to KSV,  Srimad Abhinava Ranganatha Parakala swami's work titled "Gudartha Samgraha" is a profound grantha on just first four sutras of Bramha_sutras. This work deals probably with every philosophical text in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Samkhya etc. and all the classical commentaries of Bramha sutras. This work is probably unmatched in depth during the last several hundred years since the time of Sri Vedanta Desika.  Learning under this great swamy of parakalamutt gave KSV the deep background in all Indian classical darsanas.  Incidentally, the HH PoundarIka puram Andavan  is one of the disciples of this  great scholar and acharya, Abhinava Ranganatha Parakala Swami.

However, the next level of transformation in KSV came from the divine guidance of a great yogi, Sri Ranga Maha Guru (known as Guru Bhagawan to his disciples, who hails from Hedathale, a village in Karnataka).  (Starting at 1960s or a little earlier) For 7 or 8 years KSV studied under Sriranga Maha Guru who initiated him into higher level of spiritual knowledge. KSV mentions many times that only due to the blessings of Sri Ranga Maha Guru, he could understand deep concepts of philosophy properly.

KSV told us that he has summarized what he had studied for about 70 to 80 years in an 8 page introduction to his book SarvankasA, titled "satyAnvEsana" or "in search for truth". It took me about 2 years of weekly phone calls for me to understand and translate this satyAnvEsana in english.

Interested people may also contact me for a copy of this paper.


Despite his tremendous scholarship and accomplishments, Sri KSV shies away from wealth and fame. Due to this, he initially did not want to publish a translation of his work on the Internet. He was worried that people who merely follow sampradayas blindly, without a serious quest to know the truth may not like the ideas espoused here. However, I had sent this document personally to a small set of people and got very positive feedback. In particular, Dr Saroja Ramanujam really liked this document and felt that other aspirants would also gain if this should be circulated to a wider circle. After a lot of convincing, I was finally able to convince KSV a couple of weeks ago to relent so that genuinely interested people could benefit.

Sri KSV currently lives in a small rented home in Mysore where he used to teach 16 students at his residence (recently due to old age this gurukula is is closed). It is like a small gurukula where the medium of communication is Sanskrit only. I have attached a few pictures so that people can see what it is like (this picture was taken 2 years back). It is interesting that most of the students in his class are from Nepal!  If one visits this institution, which is just a small home in Mysore, one will know how much of a sacrifice it takes to study our old granthas. Many students who study under him belong to different kalais and sampradayas; in fact one of his students is currently a sannyasi swamiji of swarnavalli matha- a shankaracharya sampradaya mutt . Some of his students are from Chaitanya sampradaya, Swaminarayan sampradaya and Vallabha sampradaya.

One of Sri KSV's main views is – ‘Vedanta cannot be monopolized by any particular sect’. In consonance with this spirit, the writing of this commentary has been a multi-sect project headed by KSV. The main students who helped write this book were: Vedantacharya Sadhu Satya prasad Das and his associate Sadhus from the Swami Narayan Sampradaya. Several others such as Vidwan Soumya Narayanan, Vidwan B.V. Ramapriya, Vidwan S. Krishnan, Sri K.V. Ramapriya M.A., Vidwan M.A. Alvar (son of M.A. Lakshmi Tatachar, former director of the Academy of Sanskrit Research, Melkote) and Chi. Ananth have worked diligently to bring this book out.

Vedantacharya Sastri Sadhu Satya Prasad Dasji Swami, belonging to Sri Swami Narayan Mandir, Bhuj, Kutch, Gujarat, has donated a computer and other accessories. The entire printing and binding cost has been funded  by Vedantacharya Sastri Sadhu Nirmal Dasji Swami. We should be grateful to all of these people who have put so much effort in getting this new commentary SarvankasA of KSV on the magnum opus of Sri Vedanta Desika: " Tattva muktA kalApa".

## वरदराजसूनुर् मणिः

I would like to share the principles behind Sri Varadacharya's approach to philosophy. For years he was a research scholar at the Oriental Research Institute in Mysore, doing fundamental work in Nyaya and Vedanta. He has published the canonical work on Jayanta Bhatta's Nyaya Manjari, for example.

Born in 1922, he trained with the legendary adhipati of the Parakala Matha in Mysore, Sri Sri Abhinava Ranganatha Parakala Swami, who insisted on an impartial, contextually sensitive study of the great Indian philosophical systems. Sri Varadacharya also received complementary guidance from some advanced yogis. His work and thinking reflects the intellectual rigor of Parakala Swami and the outcome of deep spiritual practice.

Sri Varadacharya's key points of approach can be summarized as follows:

- Indian philosophy has become a show of punditry and pedantry because of a hyper-intellectualized approach. This is a trend of over 1000 years has deeply affected what was fundamentally a spiritual tradition. 
- A slightly more recent problem is the appeal to authority, or "tradition" (sampradaya). This has unfortunately become used as a cover for ignorance, has led to a departure from thinking via first principles. Philosophy is a search for Truth and the vast majority of pandits dwell at a purely textual level and follow traditional opinions. This has greatly hampered understanding the great philosophers' true teachings.
- The great philosophers should be read with careful understanding of their historical and political contexts.
- The basis for true philosophy is broad and deep thinking, with a foundation of sincere spiritual practice.
