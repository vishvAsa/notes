What made Uttamur Swami truly remarkable was his intellectual integrity.
Even scholars of rival schools(advaitins and dwaitins) admired him because he presented arguments calmly, honestly, and without personal attacks, focusing only on philosophical clarity.

His scholarship was so respected that Advaitins and Dvaitins themselves invited him to scholarly assemblies and discussions.
Even those who disagreed with him admired his intellectual rigor. 

Vidwans of rival schools used to read his works regularly. His scholarship was so respected that Advaitins and Dvaitins themselves invited him to scholarly assemblies. Because of his immense scholarship and his defense of Visishtadvaita philosophy, scholars honored him with the title:“Abhinava Desika” — The new Vedanta Desika. A title rarely given, and fully deserved.

His lengthy, hair splitting and polemic work "Paramārtha Bhūshanam" , written to Sri Ananta Krishna Śastri as which is a criticism on Sri Vedānta Deşika Śatabhūṣaņi. this granth of his work successfully in disapproving the various doctrines of Advaita Vedanta. Anantha Krishna shastri, in one of the public gatherings dared to comment, that his community(advaitins) would have performed „Kanakabhisheka‟, a shower of gold to Uttamur swami if he would have been an advaitin.

Sri  Uttamur Swami was the very first recipient of the President's Certificate of Honour for Sanskrit scholarship it was given to him for his work Paramarth Bhushanam the work was so great that even rival school acharyas like Mahaperiyava were awe struck by the depth of this work

It is recorded that when the government sought a candidate Maha Periyava was one of the eminent Advait scholars who recommended Uttamur Swami He reportedly did so because of the immense depth and logical rigor displayed in Paramarth Bhushanam despite it being a critique of Advaita.

There is a famous anecdote that Maha Periyava once remarked that if one wanted to truly understand the finer nuances and technical depths of Advaita, they should read Uttamur Swami’s works. Such was the greatness of this work of his.

Most Vedānta scholars rarely venture into Vaiśeṣika philosophy. But Uttamur Veeraraghavacharya Swami did. His work “Vaiśeṣika Rasāyana” a masterful commentary on the Vaisheshika Sutras of Kanada, astonished contemporary scholars like maha periyava etc.

Like in Nyaya and Vaiśeşika, Uttamur Swami has done excellent commentaries on Mimāmsa Nyāya Prakaśa at his early age. It was very well appreciated by all Mimāmsa Scholars including Panditarāja D. Tatāchārya, an authority on Mimāmsa Śastra.

Commentaries on Vedānta Deşika's Seśwara Mimāmsa and Mimāmsa paduka speak his proficiency on Mimāmsa śāstra. His brief work called Upayukta Mimāmsa is a celebrated work describing the duty described in Mimāmsa śastra to be known for the good comprehension of Sri Bhāsya.

He elaborates Acharya Bhāṣya Tatparya, Sri Vedanta Deşika's commentary on Iśāvāsya Upaniṣad, by not only eliminating various criticisms, but also revealing his overwhelming scholarship in various Śastras which duly sheds light on his dialectical skills.

His mastery in various śastras like Nyāya Vaiśeşika, Mimāmsa, Yoga, Sānkhya, Advaita, Viśiṣṭādvaita, Buddhism, Jainism, etc., are easily apparent in his various works and his Tamil commentary on Vedānta Desika's Paramārtha Bhāga adds on to his credit.

He has written commentaries on almost all the works Vedant desika too HE INDEED WAS A BLESSING OF PURVACHARYAS FOR SRI VAISHNAVA SAMPRADAYA. SWAMI WITH HIS INVALUABLE PUBLICATIONS GAVE SRIVAISHNAVAS A GREAT QUANTAM LEAP IN TERMS OF THE PHILOSOPHICAL LITERATURE.

Not only a master of Sanskrit śāstras, Uttamur Veeraraghavacharya Swami was also a great expositor of the Divya Prabandham. His detailed commentaries illuminated the divine hymns of the Alvars for Sri Vaishnavas.


Uttamur swami was a strict follower of Anushthnas. He lived his like just as recommended by shastras and Sri Vedant Desika. He never longed for fame and led a simple life. He was humble and believed the life to be rendered on the service of Lord Lakshmi Narayan and their devotees.
