However, the complex construction of this long sentence in the inscription leads one to ask the question- was the year тАШKrodhiтАЩ 1346 of the salivahana saka the year that Annam─Бch─Бrya was born? Or was it the year when Lord V├кnkat├кshwara manifested to him?

Some opine that this was the year of his birth, and thus they indicate the year 1424 C.E. as the birth year of Annam─Бch─Бrya.

However, careful observation of the copper plate inscription indicates that the word тАШpratyaksamaitenuтАЩ тАУ тАШbecame manifestтАЩ is the verb ending that becomes qualified by preceding portion of the long sentence. Thus, is it more appropriate to take the year 1424 C.E. as the year the Lord manifested to Sri Annam─Бch─Бrya. His birth therefore would have been in the year 1408 C.E., sixteen years preceding this event. The period Saka 1346 to 1424 (i.e., 1424 C.E. to 1503 C.E.) therefore denotes the period of his steadfastness to carrying out the divine mandate of the Lord, by composing Sankirtanas dedicated to Him. Indeed, looking at the prologues for the works of his son Tirumalacharya and grandson Chinna Tirumalacharya also reveals that the dates mentioned in the corresponding references denotes only the period of dedicating their lives in composing Sankirtanas on the Lord, and not their dates of birth.
