कृतिस् तस्यां मध्व-तन्त्र-चन्द्रिका-वन-मालयोः ।  
वेदान्ताचार्य-रचिता शत-दूषणि-संज्ञिका ॥ १२ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

कृतिस् तस्यां दोड्डयार्य-रचिते चण्ड-मारुते ।  
तत्तन्-मतस्थ-भाष्येषु तथा विद्यान्तरेषु च ॥ १३ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

in his work, and in Chanda-Maruta composed by Doddayarya, and in the commentaries of those respective schools, and also in other branches of knowledge,
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

कृतिस् tasyāṁ doḍḍayārya-racite caṇḍa-mārute ।  
tattat-matastha-bhāṣyeṣu tathā vidyāntareṣu ca ॥ १३ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

ज्ञानं यस्य महान्तोऽपि शास्त्रिणस् त्र्यम्बकादयः ।  
ज्ञात्वाश्चर्यं मुदं चैव प्राप्ता इति हि शुश्रुमः ॥ १४ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

we have indeed heard that even great scholars like Tryambaka and others, upon knowing his (Anna's son Narayana's) knowledge, attained both wonder and joy.
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

ज्ञानं yasya mahānto’pi śāstriṇas tryambakādayaḥ ।  
jñātvāścaryam mudaṁ caiva prāptā iti hi śuśrumaḥ ॥ १४ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

सीता-पतेः पाद-पद्मे यन्-मनोऽलिः सदा स्थितः ।  
इति श्रुतं मया तस्य पुत्रः श्री-रामशंकरः ॥ १५ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

Whose mind, like a bee, was always fixed on the lotus feet of Sita's Lord (Rama) – thus I have heard; his son is Sri Ramashankara.
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

sītā-pateḥ pāda-padme yan-mano’liḥ sadā sthitaḥ ।  
iti śrutaṁ mayā tasya putraḥ śrī-rāmaśaṅkaraḥ ॥ १५ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

धर्म-शास्त्रे तथा पूर्व-तन्त्रे बुद्धिम् अवाप सः ।  
तस्य पुत्र-त्रयं तत्र ज्येष्ठो नारायणाह्वयः ॥ १६ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

He attained proficiency in Dharma-shastra and Purva-Tantra (Mimamsa). He had three sons; among them, the eldest was named Narayana.
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

dharma-śāstre tathā pūrva-tantre buddhim avāpa saḥ ।  
tasya putra-trayaṁ tatra jyeṣṭho nārāyaṇāhvayaḥ ॥ १६ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

श्रुति-स्मृति-तद्-अर्थेषु सम्यक्-श्रद्धा-समन्वितः ।  
कोदण्ड-पूर्व-कैर् वर्णैर् नाम्ना रामेति विश्रुतः ॥ १७ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

He was endowed with proper faith in Shruti, Smriti, and their meanings. He was renowned by the name Rama, with the letters preceding "Kodanda".
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

śruti-smṛti-tad-artheṣu samyak-śraddhā-samanvitaḥ ।  
kodaṇḍa-pūrva-kair varṇair nāmnā rāmeti viśrutaḥ ॥ १७ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

कनिष्ठो मध्यमस् तत्र राम-सुब्ब्व् इति विश्रुतः ।  
शिव-रामार्थ-संज्ञानां देशिकानां महात्मनाम् ॥ १८ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

The youngest and middle one there was known as Rama-Subba. From the great teachers named Shivaramartha,
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

kaniṣṭho madhyamas tatra rāma-subbbv iti viśrutaḥ ।  
śiva-rāmārtha-saṁjñānāṁ deśikānāṁ mahātmanām ॥ १८ ॥
</details>

<details open><summary>विश्वास-प्रस्तुतिः</summary>

प्रसादात् सर्व-शास्त्रज्ञो राम-दास्य-रसानुभूः ।  
तेनायं रचितो ग्रन्थो लोकानुग्रह-काम्यया ॥ १९ ॥
</details>

<details><summary>En - Gemini 2.5</summary>

by their grace, he became omniscient in all scriptures, and experienced the bliss of servitude to Rama. This work was composed by him with the desire to benefit the world.
</details>


<details><summary>मूलम्</summary>

prasādāt sarva-śāstrajño rāma-dāsya-rasānubhūḥ ।  
tenāyaṁ racito grantho lokānugraha-kāmyayā ॥ १९ ॥
</details>

**इति विष्णु-द्वेषकर-महा-शैव-मत-मर्दनं संपूर्णम् ।**
