Rūpa and Sanātana were powerful, wealthy, and highly learned ministers in the government of the Sultan of Bengal, Hussain Shāh. Sanātana was the Prime Minister (Sakara Mallika) and Rūpa was the Private Secretary (Dabir Khas).

## Meeting 1514
In 1514 CE, during his tour to Vṛndāvana (via Bengal), Caitanya Mahāprabhu stopped at their village of Ramakeli.

Sanātana Gosvāmī (born c. 1488): He was approximately 26 years old when he first met Caitanya Mahāprabhu in 1514.  
Rūpa Gosvāmī (born c. 1493): He was approximately 21 years old at that same first meeting.

Caitanya accepted them and gave them their life's mission: renounce their worldly positions, go to Vṛndāvana, and there, (1) excavate the lost holy places, (2) install Deities and establish proper worship, and (3) write the definitive theological texts for the new movement.

(1 year later).  

Rūpa managed to resign first, taking their accumulated wealth and strategically distributing it (to family, brāhmaṇas, and for securing Sanātana's release). Sanātana's attempt to resign angered the Sultan, who had him imprisoned. Sanātana famously escaped by bribing his jailer with the money Rūpa had set aside for him.
