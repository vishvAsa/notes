## Double standards
We live in an era where "cultural erasure" of indigenous peoples is considered a major crime in Western academia, yet when it comes to the "pagan" history of India that same erasure is defended as "secular statecraft."

## Aurangzeb whitewashing
Ironically, if he had only oppressed Bohras, Shias, Pashtuns, etc., Awrangzeb would've been regarded as a bigot.

The fact that he was much more oppressive towards Hindus (and, to a lesser extent, Sikhs and Jains), as recorded in both foreign and native accounts, ironically saved him from being labeled as bigot.

It led to a reactionary whitewashing by Indian historians affiliated with the Aligarh School. In post-independence Indian historiography, admitting that a major Mughal emperor was a fundamentalist bigot was seen as a grave threat to national unity.
