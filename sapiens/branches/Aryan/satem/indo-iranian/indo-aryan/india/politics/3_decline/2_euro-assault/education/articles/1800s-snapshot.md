The Madras Presidency Survey (1822–25) documented 12,498 schools across 21 districts, nearly one for every 1.3 villages & education was not barred to any caste.. Infact

> Out of 808 studying Astronomy, only 78 were Brahmins.
> Out of 194 studying Medicine, just 31 were Brahmins.
> In Rajahmundry, even in higher education, scholars were Sudras. 🤷‍♀️

William Adam’s Bengal reports (1835–38) found schools in almost every 3rd to 4th village, teaching reading, arithmetic, law, logic, and astronomy. G.W. Leitner recorded 1,600 schools in Lahore district & declared that Punjab was “more educated than England.” .. Meanwhile, in 1818 England, only 478,000 of 1.25 million children were in school most in Sunday schools, teaching only Bible reading.

Pre colonial India did have indigenous schools with teachers and students from multiple castes, even Shudra instructors in some regions. Colonial restructuring weakened many of these local systems, and since overall literacy was extremely low, illiteracy at independence wasn’t unique to any single caste.
