A horrifying story from  
@Aham_Bharatiya
  family during their escape from East Bengal.

January 1972. Just weeks after Bangladesh was born.

My father took my newly married Mejo Jethima to Dhaka and Jessore so she could offer prayers to the Kul Devis of both families. She married into our family on 15 December 1971.

What she saw shattered her forever.

In Jessore, our Muslim neighbour had occupied our ancestral home while the family was in Kolkata for the wedding. Our Kobiraji shop was seized. The house was looted. Everything destroyed.

And when that man saw her return, his leering eyes filled her with fear and disgust. Even today, decades later, she trembles while narrating that moment.

Then they went to Bikrampur, Dhaka.

Same story again.

Another occupied ancestral home.
Another erased Hindu family.

But this wasn’t the first time.

Back in 1960, my father’s Chhoto Mashi and Chhoto Mama had to flee Dhaka to save their lives.

My young aunt was so beautiful that the family feared she would be kidnapped and raped on the way. So they wrapped her entire body in bandages to flatten her figure, painted her skin black to hide her fair complexion, and made her lie silently like a dead body while boarding the Howrah-bound train.

Why?

Because otherwise mobs could rape her, loot their money and gold, and kill the entire family.

My uncle bandaged himself to look like a leprosy patient so nobody would come near him.

These are not “stories”.
These are living memories carried by countless Bengali Hindu refugee families.

And then people ask:
“Why did Bengali Hindus leave East Bengal?”

History remembers.
Even if politics wants silence.
