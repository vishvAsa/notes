Bhimjee Parakh, a broker of the east India company, set up a printing press in Bombay in 1674 to print 'ancient Brahminy writings'. It got delayed as a type-founder was only available in 1678. He and his brother Kalyan Parakh appear very devout Hindus, who tried to put forth the Hindu perspectives when confronted by criticisms from the Christian side.
