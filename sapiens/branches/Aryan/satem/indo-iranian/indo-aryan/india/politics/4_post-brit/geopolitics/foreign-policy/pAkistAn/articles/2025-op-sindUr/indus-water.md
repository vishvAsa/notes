## Goal
The goal is not to withold 80%. That is not possible.
The goal is to build new reservoirs, canal links, irrigation projects, etc. so that it can withhold an additional 15-20%.  
Obviously all water flowing into Pakistan cannot simply be turned off immediately, but it's fair for India to build reservoirs, canals, pumping systems, and recharge infrastructure to divert much of the water.



Regarding India's suspension of the Indus Water Treaty following Pahalgam terrorist attack, I used to think it was solely punitive but my current view is that it is justified.

India already had the absolute right to use 100% of the water from the Ravi under the IWT yet it didn't complete the Shahpur Kandi Barrage until this year.

Moreover, the Chenab-Beas Link Tunnel is not only feasible but can be completed within the span of seven to ten years.

The Lahaul-Spiti valley is tough to access but there are established mountain passes like the Atal Tunnel right next door.


## Pak punjab
Moreover, Pakistani Punjab is semiarid and the crop yield per hectare is lower than in Indian states like Indian Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh etc.

Irrigation is needed to compensate for insufficient/unreliable rainfall, so if irrigation supplies decline then the same would be true for yields of water-intensive crops.


## Indian punjab
The aquifers in Indian Punjab are severely overexploited and diverting river water could help replenish Punjab's dying aquifers to some degree. This may sound cynical, but some degree of food insecurity and inflation in Pakistan is ultimately good for India because it places Pakistan under resource constraints.

## New constraints on Pak
It forces the Pakistani government to allocate funds for food, infrastructure, etc. rather than funding cross-border terrorist proxies in Kashmir.

Moreover, forcing Pakistan to rely on IMF bailouts is ultimately good because its financial systems are subjected to stricter scrutiny and this ultimately makes it more difficult to divert state funds for covert operations & militant proxies.

## Neighbors
It's not as if neighboring countries have been entirely fair to India either. Electricity lines, highway lines, crude oil pipelines, fiber optic cables for telecommunication, military garrisons, etc. are forced to pass through the narrow 22 km wide Siliguri corridor.

This makes projects like Manas-Sankosh-Teesta-Ganga Link Project difficult without support from neighboring countries like Bangladesh, yet Bangladesh is fiercely protective of its border.

Given that neighboring countries ultimately care about their own interests they don't feel any obligation to show goodwill to their neighbors, it only make sense that India should also prioritize its own interests and security concerns.

India has a legal right to retain more water and has underutilized its eastern-river entitlements. Why should it indefinitely provide benefits to a hostile Western neighbour whose sworn policy is to use terrorist proxies & asymmetric warfare to "bleed India by 1000 cuts"?

## Imports
Pakistan is not Sudan or Yemen.
It has a large agricultural sector and the ability to import food if the need arises. Some water diversion not going to result in an immediate crisis in Pakistan since Pakistan has the capacity to absorb it.

## War
India is actually playing its cards wisely here, all things considered.
Pakistan is highly constrained when it comes to launching a full-scale conventional war over temporary suspension of the Indus Water Treaty.
This is not 1965 or 1971.

### Oil
Full-scale conventional wars burn through billions of dollars daily & Pakistan's foreign exchange reserves are critically low.

Pakistan also relies entirely on imported oil to fuel its tanks, fighter jets, transport trucks, etc. and in the case of a full-scale conventional war, India's navy could theoretically enforce a maritime blockade on Karachi.
India has several operational aircraft carriers like INS Vikramaditya and INS Vikrant, so the Indian Navy can deploy at least one Carrier Strike Group to monitor the North Arabian Sea.

India could declare a Maritime Exclusion Zone on any commercial oil tanker or cargo ship attempting to sail toward Pakistan & MiG-29K figher jets could scout & intercept commercial vessels trying to reach Karachi or Gwadar hundreds of miles before they approach land.
India also has access to long-range maritime patrol aircrafts like Boeing P-81 Poseidon and Scorpene stealth submarines which are superior to Pakistan's Agosta-class submarines and 039B Hangor-class submarines.
India also has the capacity to lay naval mines to the South & pin the Pakistani surface fleet inside their bases.


### Budget
Moreover, India's defense budget is $75-80 billion while Pakistan is only around $6-7 billion, so India can absorb losses of equipment, ammunition etc. more efficiently than Pakistan can.
In addition, Pakistan is weary of a two-front war.
It can't risk a full-scale war with India when its Western border with Afghanistan is volatile.

## China water
Pakistan also cannot ask China to "turn off the tap" to India in retaliation since China cannot do so without taking immense risks itself.

### Drilling
It would have to drill along the Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis, which is one of the most seismically unstable regions in the world.

On the other hand, China cannot safely or reliably drill on top of the Eastern Himalayan Syntaxis because it's one of the deadliest seismic zones on the planet.
Moreover, the Yarlung Tsangpo Grand Canyon is the deepest canyon on Earth and is three times deeper than the Grand Canyon.
It's so rugged, isolated, & vertical that basic roads into Medog County didn't exist until the 2010s.

The Chinese are likely going to bypass the loop where the river whips around Mount Namcha Barwa when constructing the hydropower dam.

It's clear that India has much more direct leverage over Pakistan through Indus tributaries than China has over India through the Brahmaputra.

### Bangladesh
Even if it somehow managed to control the outflow against all odds, Bangladesh is the lowest riparian and would suffer the most, NOT Assam or Northeast India.
China is not willing to take the risk of abruptly severing relations with Bangladesh. So the China-card is ultimately a bluff. 

### Non-essential
Additionally, India doesn't rely on the Brahmaputra in the same way as Pakistan relies on the Indus.
India has multiple massive river systems (Ganges, Brahmaputra, Godavari, etc.) while Pakistan is entirely a one-river economy.

More than 80% of agricultural land in Pakistan is irrigated by the Indus or its tributaries. So even if India were to permanently divert 15% of water via canal networks into Indian Punjab or Rajathan, it could have noticeable effects on the total agricultural output of Pakistan.

## Other China support
### north Korea case
It's also not true that China has a "blank check" poicy for Pakistan.
China doesn't treat Islamabad in the same way as it treats Pyongyang.
China is an endless bank/pantry/gas station for North Korea because China treats North Korea as an insurance policy.
North Korea is a "shield" which protects China's industrial heartland in Manchuria from the US & the West.
If the North Korean regime were to suddenly collapse one day, China would have a unified, democratic Korea backed by United States and NATO military right at its doorstep.

### Non threat
On the contrary, India is not an immediate threat to China. 
India superficially appears close to China, but India actually borders the TAR and Burma.

India is remote from the actual Chinese "heartland" (i.e. the Central Plains) & can't pose an existential crisis for China in the same way that Korea or Japan can. Both India and China are aware of this fact.


Chengdu in Sichuan province is the closest hyper-dense mega-region (Kunming in Yunnan Province is populous, but not hyper-dense) & although the straight line distance from the LOC in Arunachal Pradesh is merely 587 miles, the Tibetan Plateau and the Himalayan mountain range force a detour through Yunnan Province, Myanmar, and India via a winding route.

So the actually driving distance increases to around 4,800 kilometers or 3,000 miles.

In other words, China's Western Theatre Command headquarters in Chengdu is out of reach of any frontline threats from India.

So Pakistan is clearly not as vital a strategic asset for China as North Korea.

### Lending conditions
Beijing can and *does* set conditions on investment and loans. Beijing is ultimately a commercial lender for Islamabad and not a charity.
It has demanded return on investment and Pakistan has not been able to meet those demands.
One of the main reasons Main Line-1 railway upgrade was delayed was because Chinese engineers, personnel, etc. who were employed in CPEC projects in Balochistan and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa were targeted by terrorist groups like the Balochistan Liberation Army.
China generally prefers that Pakistan seek IMF bailouts so China doesn't lose its investments when Pakistan inevitably defaults.

China won't write a multi-billion dollar check to fund agricultural overhaul in Pakistan, so Pakistan will therefore have to divert funds from the IMF and its own reserves towards agricultural overhaul over the next decade (research and development for drought-resistant GMO varieities, cutting down on water-guzzling exports like sugar cane and rice in favor of millets, pulses, oilseed, shift from flood irrigation towards drip irrigation, etc.). If Pakistan fails to do so over the next decade, farmers in Punjab and Sindh will suffer and so will citizens.

This reallocation of funds means fewer funds would be directed towards the proxy-war/war of attrition in Kashmir and India's ultimate goal is to squeeze Pakistan's ability to fund proxies.
