Even if you accept the conservative, "low" estimate of 300,000 rather than 3,000,000, the death toll during the Bangladesh Genocide is nearly double the combined immediate fatalities of both the Hiroshima and Nagasaki atomic bombings.

This massacre took place over 9 months, so even if we accept the lower estimate, the daily death toll over that period is roughly the same as under the Khmer Rouge (~1.7 million killed over a period of 4 years).

It's not as high as during the Rwandan Genocide or during Operation Reinhard (a three-month window during which ~25% of all Holocaust victims were murdered), but those are some of the most "efficient" instances of mass murder in human history.

If we recognize a 4-year genocide with a daily toll of ~1,300 (Cambodia), there is no logical reason to deny a 9-month genocide that killed at least the same amount per day (lower, more conservative estimates) and potentially ten times more.
