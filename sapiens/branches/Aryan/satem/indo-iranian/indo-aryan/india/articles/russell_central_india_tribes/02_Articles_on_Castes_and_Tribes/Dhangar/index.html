The traditional occupation of the Dhangars is to tend sheep and goats, and they also sell goatsâ€™ milk, make blankets from the wool of sheep, and sometimes breed and sell stock for slaughter. Their dress consists frequently only of a loin-cloth and a blanket, and having to bear exposure to all weathers, they are naturally strong and hardy. In appearance they are dark and of medium size. They eat three times a day and bathe in the evening on returning from work, though their ablutions are sometimes omitted in the cold weather.
