## Dose
Adults need ~0.9 mg/day of copper;  
excess (>10 mg/day) can be harmful.  

## Overdose effects 
Reproductive harm – oxidative stress can impair sperm quality. cuproptosis (copper-induced cell death) vs sperm.

Brain damage – from prolonged accumulation.

Liver damage – cirrhosis or failure in chronic cases.

Kidney failure – inability to filter waste.

Heart failure – rare but possible in extreme overload.

## Elimination
Liver clearance	Liver mobilizes stored copper into bile	Days 2–7  
Blood normalization	Serum copper and ceruloplasmin stabilize	~1–2 weeks  
Full tissue balance	Organs return to baseline copper levels	2–3 weeks  
1–3 months	New sperm cycles (spermatogenesis takes ~74 days) restore motility and morphology.  
3–6 months	Full recovery of sperm count and DNA integrity if no chronic damage.

## Water in copper vessels 
Ok with short (4 hrs) storage.  
Possible excess -  

- Stored overnight
- acidic liquids

Cooking or eating in copper vessel is a bad idea - leaching risks.  
Brass vessels used to be coated with tin.
