Many people seem  to be curious about the old hag phenomenon. In short, it is a peculiar experience where you are undergoing sleep paralysis or a comparable state where one is unable to respond, when the figure of an old hag or a violent, gray-haired woman with disheveled hair comes and sits on your abdomen or chest (folk mantra-traditions see it as jyeShThA or dhUmAvatI or their agents). 

It is often accompanied by a combination of one or more of the following:  
a sense of dread, choking or an inability to breathe or move.  
Eventually, one awakes, and it vanishes. 

The most notable and mysterious part of this experience is why people across different cultures who have never previously read about the phenomenon consistently experience it in a similar manner. It is possible that some people see the old hag more vividly than others. 

David Hufford's book is good place to start: https://scholarworks.iu.edu/dspace/bitstreams/7d78e13f-d48a-4651-8237-2db534f0baad/download

After studying this across Indian, Chinese and Europoid American percipients, I've come to the conclusion that while the OH phenomenon is accompanied by something similar to sleep paralysis, not all of the latter produce OH. Hence, it is an open question why OH emerges, that too across distinct cultures with the same imagery. There is something more here -- neurological or ghostly.


## Experience
This is not about H views on sleep paralysis, but just a comment. I have had sleep paralysis once in a while, and it is unpleasant and memorable. Most people report it as such. However, only on one occasion did I experience the "Old Hag" phenomenon. 

While I don't have vivid or memorable dreams on most nights, the OH experience that I had was extremely vivid.
