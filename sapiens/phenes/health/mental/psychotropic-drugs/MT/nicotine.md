Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2073616194276393467)

In Central-Western Australia, extending both northwards and southwards, the aboriginal tribes make a preparation called Pituri from a broadly Datura-like plant known as Duboisia. They dry the leaves, powder them and mix them with wood ash (to raise the pH to free the alkaloids). Alongside the alkaloid Scopolamine, the plant also has nicotine and nornicotine, which together are supposed to give the user stimulating and hunger-suppressing effects. This, along with the desert tobaccos, represents an independent discovery of nicotine by tribal populations.
