## Fertility window
The egg is viable for 12–24 hours after ovulation. Sperm can live up to 5 days in the female reproductive tract.

Focus on identifying your fertile window (the 5–6 days leading up to and including ovulation) and having sex every 1–2 days during it.

Best timing: The highest pregnancy probability occurs in the 2 days before ovulation and on ovulation day itself. Sex every day or every other day throughout the ~6-day window gives excellent coverage without needing to pinpoint the exact hour.


## Defence mechanisms
To some extant, fertility is reduced, rather than deformity increased.

Natural selection barriers: 

- Only the healthiest sperm usually reach and fertilize the egg. Damaged sperm are far less likely to succeed.
- Even if an unhealthy sperm reaches, miscarriage chances increase.

Egg repair mechanisms: The egg has DNA repair systems that can correct minor sperm DNA damage.

## Risks
When Risks Increase

Severe oxidative stress: If sperm DNA damage is extensive, it may increase miscarriage risk or lower IVF success rates.

Chronic exposure: Long-term copper overload can impair sperm production cycles, reducing overall fertility.

Combined factors: Smoking, alcohol, or other toxins compound risks.

### Age
- A study published in the Archives of General Psychiatry reveals that men over 40 are six times more likely to father a child with autism than men aged 15 to 29. \[[Ref](http://www.smart-kit.com/s143/dont-wait-too-long-to-father-children/)\]

#### Male
- A French study of over 12,200 couples having fertility treatment suggests the chance of a successful pregnancy falls when the man is aged over 35. It adds that the chance is significantly lower if he is over 40. \[[Ref](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/health/7492323.stm)\]  
  

#### Female
Age 40 -   
Around 5% chance per menstrual cycle (roughly 1 in 20), with cumulative chances of ~40-50%+ over 12 months of frequent unprotected sex.

Miscarriage risk rises significantly with age (~25-50%+ at 40 vs. lower in 20s/30s) due to egg quality.



## Weight
The heaviest men had a higher proportion of abnormal sperm, as well as other problems. \[[Ref](http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/health/7494777.stm)\]

### Toxicity
Oxidative damage. Eg. from excess copper.


## Supplements
Maintain a healthy weight (already lean helps), take a prenatal vitamin (with folic acid), consider CoQ10 (discuss with doctor for egg quality support), reduce alcohol/caffeine, quit smoking, and manage stress. Moderate exercise is beneficial.
