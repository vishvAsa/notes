A gene associated with pyschical experience?

We have long held that what in the West is seen as psychical experience has a genetic component. Both the ability to exhibit such capabilities and to have such experiences have a familial basis. 


Source: [TW](https://sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1550830726001035?via%3Dihub)  
A relatively obscure study was published in this regard. This study was performed using commercial genetic analysis on educated white American women, who claimed to have high "paranormal" beliefs and "moderate levels of psychic experiences", with 82% of them reporting such traits in other family members. It identified 212 SNPs significantly associated with psychic experience, with the strongest association for rs10410488, mapping to the gene ARHGEF18, which codes for a Rho GTPase guanine nucleotide exchange factor. 

This gene is widely expressed across most major tissues and is not particularly specific to the brain or neural function, as claimed by the original authors reporting it. In fact, it has the highest expression in immune tissues like the thymus, lymph nodes and spleen. Nor are there any other indications of the association of this gene with neuropsychiatric effects. 

Hence, while we do agree that it might be support for the genetic basis for psychical experience, it is still unclear why that is the case.
