## Success rate
Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2038453860335820852)

One thing the wars in Ukraine and the Islamic Republic have shown is that none of the prognosticators get things right to a high degree. In both cases, we find: 

1. Those who get certain broad contours or elements right (e.g., Mearsheimer) but fail in prognosticating the actual course. 
2. Those who overpitch or underpitch with great confidence: claim complete victory for one or the other side. These fail badly, yet continue to selectively keep seeing things that match their perspective.
3. Those who get certain specific battles or phases right. While their prognosis does not work in totality, they seem to have a good short-term prognosis.

In conclusion, it is mostly like astrology and the astrologers. I tend to see life probabilistically. Even if it were deterministic deep down (we do not think so sensu strictu), for the lesser mortal, it manifests as a vector of probabilities. 

The general failure to achieve a good prognosis shows that estimating those probabilities is rather difficult for most people, whether through special knowledge or intuition. Those who have studied the evolution of strange fractal attractors know that this is not entirely surprising.
