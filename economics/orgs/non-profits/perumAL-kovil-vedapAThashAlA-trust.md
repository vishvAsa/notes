## Form 10AD 
### Benefits
*   **Tax Exemption on Trust Income:** This registration is the foundation of tax exemption for a non-profit in India. It means that the income the trust receives (including voluntary contributions and donations) will **not be taxed**, provided the trust utilizes the funds for its stated religious and charitable purposes.


### Key Conditions the Trust Must Follow (Pages 3 & 4)
To maintain this tax-exempt status over the 10-year period, the trust must adhere to several rules listed in the Annexure:
1.  **Strict Use of Funds:** All income must be used solely for the charitable and religious objects of the trust.
2.  **No Commercial Activity:** The trust cannot run a business for profit unless it is directly incidental to its main charitable objective, and separate books of accounts must be kept for it.
3.  **No Private Benefit:** Funds cannot be used for private religious/personal benefits.
4.  **Geographic Limit:** The funds must be utilized within India, unless specific approval is obtained from the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT).
5.  **Irrevocability:** The trust is irrevocable, meaning the founders cannot take back the assets or funds once they are committed to the trust.


### 80G Refusal
It is important to note **Condition No. 9** on Page 4 of the document:

> *"The registration u/s.12AB of the Income Tax Act, 1961 does not automatically confer any right on the donors to claim deduction u/s.80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961."*

*   **What this means:** While this document (Section 12AB) makes the *trust’s* income tax-free, it **does not** automatically allow *donors* to claim tax deductions on the money they donate.
*   To give donors a tax deduction, the trust must apply for and receive a separate approval under **Section 80G** of the Income Tax Act.

#### Rejection order

This document is an official **Order of Rejection** issued by the **Income Tax Department (Chennai)** on **March 26, 2026**. It formally rejects the application filed by **Perumal Koil Vedapatasala Trust** seeking approval under **Section 80G(5)** of the Income Tax Act, 1961 (which would have allowed donors to claim tax deductions on their contributions).

---

#### **Core Reason for Rejection**
The Commissioner of Income Tax (Exemption), Premanand Jothy, rejected the application on the grounds that the trust is **not established "solely" for charitable purposes**, but is a composite **"Religious cum Charitable"** trust [1.1.4, 1.2.1].

The specific legal arguments used by the department include:

1.  **Religious Nature of Objects:** Based on the trust deed and financial submissions, some of the trust's core objectives and expenses are explicitly religious in nature (associated with running the Vedapatasala and temple activities) [1.2.1]. Under Explanation 3 to Section 80G, a "charitable purpose" does not include purposes that are wholly or substantially religious [1.1.1, 1.2.1].
2.  **Ineligibility under Section 80G(5):** The department states that 80G registration can only be granted to institutions established *strictly* for charitable purposes. Trusts established for "Religious" or "Religious cum Charitable" purposes are excluded from this benefit [1.1.4, 1.2.1].
3.  **Strict Interpretation of the 5% Spending Rule:** The department clarified that the statutory allowance allowing a trust to spend up to 5% of its income on religious activities **only applies if the trust is established solely for charitable purposes** in its deed [1.1.1, 1.1.4, 1.2.6]. Because this trust's deed explicitly defines its nature as "religious cum charitable," the department ruled that it fails this entry-level condition entirely, regardless of the actual percentage of funds spent on religious vs. charitable activities [1.1.1, 1.1.4, 1.2.1].

### **Next Steps / Rights of Appeal**
Since this order is dated **March 26, 2026**, the trust has the legal right to challenge this rejection:
*   **Appeal Authority:** An appeal can be filed before the **Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (ITAT)** [1.1.2, 1.1.5].
*   **Timeline:** The appeal must be filed using **Form No. 36** within **60 days** from the date on which this rejection order was communicated to the trust.
*   **Common Defense in Appeal:** Many similarly situated Vedapatasalas have successfully appealed such rejections in the ITAT by demonstrating that imparting Vedic and Sanskrit education constitutes "Education" (a recognized charitable purpose under Section 2(15) of the Act), rather than purely dogmatic religious rituals.
