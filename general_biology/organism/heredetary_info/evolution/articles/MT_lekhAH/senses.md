## Acoustics

Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2036669681084297409)

Darwin postulated that human preferences for the beautiful, for example, in form, color and sound is shared with other non-human animals. Thus, he conjectured that the human sense of beauty is an expression of an ancestral sentiment with deep roots in animals. A recent study finds support for this in terms of  "beautiful sounds" preferred by humans and other animals. That explains why humans like the bird songs, for instance. Indeed, our ancestors saw the sAman-s as equivalent to the bird song, as gR^itsamada says: "udgAteva shakune sAma gAyasi"
