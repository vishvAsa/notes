* The heaviest dinosaur, Argentinosaurus, is estimated to be at least 60-65 tons in mass.
* One of the lightest dinosaurs, the Ruby-throated hummingbird, is about 3 grams. 
* Hence, the same basic dinosaurian body plan allows for a mass scaling factor of about 20-21 million.

* The blue whale is about 130 tons on average.
* The lightest mammal, the Etruscan Shrew, is about 1.8 grams
* Hence, the same basic mammalian body plan allows for a mass scaling factor of about 72 million.

Thus, both the dinosaurian and mammalian body plans allowed scaling over 7-8 orders of magnitude. However, the dinosaurs achieved this entirely on land with no buoyancy assist.  However, the most massive land mammal was the extinct rhinoceros Paraceratherium at 15-18 tons. It was thus only 10 million times the mass of the lightest mammal.
