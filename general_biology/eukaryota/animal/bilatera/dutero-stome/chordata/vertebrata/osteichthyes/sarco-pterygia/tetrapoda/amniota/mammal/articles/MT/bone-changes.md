A [good way](https://digitallibrary.amnh.org/items/dbbf9dc2-f0cb-40fc-87b2-a92791f3c3cc) of illustrating the anatomy of the lizard skull using the skull of the gigantic marine lizard Tylosaurus rex, recently described by Zietlow et al. Unfortunately, two bones are not labeled at the extreme posterior end of the cranium and the mandible. The light violet articular and the dark brown quadrate.

The lizard retains the 7 bones of the ancestral reptilian lower jaw. In mammals, the articular and the quadrate have become ear bones, while the dentary has become the sole bone of the lower jaw. Mammals and reptiles appear to have optimized respectively for strength from rigidity vs flexibility.
