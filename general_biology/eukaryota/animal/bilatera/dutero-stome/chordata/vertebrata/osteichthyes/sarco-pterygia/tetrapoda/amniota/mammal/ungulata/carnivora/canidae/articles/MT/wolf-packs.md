## Wolf packs
Wolf packs are family units where the dominant reproductive pair, the patriarch and matriarch, are supported by the rest of their pack, mostly made up of their offspring and sometimes more distant relatives (or more rarely, unrelated adoptees). Wolf fathers commonly:

* Guard pups.
* Bring them food.
* Regurgitate food for pups that have stopped drinking milk.
* Participate actively in their social development.

## Dhole
A similar situation is seen in the dhole, where both parents and other pack members actively participate in rearing the pups.

## Golden jackal
This probably evolved from an ancestor system like that of a golden jackal. These are married for life as a monogamous pair. Their offspring sometimes stick around for some time with the parents and help them raise their younger siblings, and noticeably improve their chances of survival. 

## Solitary jackal
As an aside, the solitary jackal might symbiotically associate with a tiger and scavenge the remains of its kills. In return, it vocalizes to warn the tiger of rivals or kill-snatchers like dholes, which are locked in a life-and-death conflict with them. viShNusharman's accounts lead us to speculate that in old India the jackal might have perhaps formed such an association with lions.+++(5)+++

## Dog
When the wolf was domesticated into the cur, its family structure was destroyed. The fathers are minimally or entirely uninvolved in family life. They often do not recognize their pups and might even end up killing them. The entire responsibility of their upbringing falls on the bitch, and their socialization in the household breeds is mostly dependent on the human owner – the life of a dog or shvavR^itti as our ancestors would say.
