title = "Sweat milk"
+++
Source: [TW](https://x.com/blog_supplement/status/2060936656606085397)

Mammalian milk ultimately evolved from sweat.  
First, there emerged a special sweat with anti-microbial proteins that, at some point, stem mammals used to hydrate their eggs,  
which, unlike dinosaur eggs, were leathery and could dry up.  
We still see that in the egg-laying mammals, the platypi and echidna, which have a milk protein that originally worked as an antimicrobial in the mucosa. 

This hydrating and antiseptic protein-rich sweat seems to have been used as a nutrient by the hatchlings, eventually giving rise to dedicated milk.
