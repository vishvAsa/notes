In the 1800s, during the English tyranny, the growing filth in the city of Kolkata was kept clean by the voracious Greater adjutant stork, which consumed everything from half-cremated human corpses to garbage lying around the city. These storks did more good for the city than the pretabhAnakI ever did, and the English overlords rightly acknowledged them by placing them on their coat of arms as they were seen all around Kolkata. 

However, post-independence, they declined to the point of endangerment, and only more recently have they begun recovering in Assam. We suspect that Mesozoic unenlagiine dromaeosaurs, like Austroraptor, were probably similar in ecology.
