In the below preprint, Reich tries to provide a unified view for the origin of Neanderthals, Denisovans and sapiens centered on the expansion of the Levallois stone tool-producing population. It is elegant in its simplicity. 

As per his model, this Levallois population mixed with a very ancient African lineage that had diverged from them around 1.5 Mya in an 80+20 admixture, giving rise to sapiens. The Levallois lineage split from a lineage that entered Eurasia around 750 Kya. 

That archaic Eurasian lineage split into the branches eventually leading to the Neanderthals and Denisovans around 550 Kya. 

The Levallois population mixed with the former of those Eurasian populations in a 5+95 admixture, giving rise to the Neanderthals. The latter of those populations mixed with a "superarchaic" lineage, giving rise to the Denisovans proper. However, the real challenge that remains is how this scenario can be mapped onto the rest of the fossil record:

1. Is the rhodesiensis-heidelbergensis split the one corresponding to that between the lineage leading to the Levallois lineage and the archaic Eurasian lineage?

2. Who do the African archaics correspond to? How do they relate to Homo rudolfensis (likely candidate?), Homo naledi or some other undiscovered lineage?

3. What about the range of East Asian archaics? Are they a part of the radiation of the stem lineage of the Denisovans?

4. What about the superarchaic who admixed with the Denisovan lineage: are they connected to Homo erectus, the Dmanisi, Georgia erectus-like Homo, or some other group yet undiscovered?

5. How does the Narmada cranium fit in all this -- an archaic of the stem Denisovan lineage, or a population of heidelbergensis, or a superarchaic?
