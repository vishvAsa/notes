The formal publication on the great Chimpanzee civil war in Uganda is out: https://science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adz4944
A large band of chimps broke up into two warring factions. The smaller faction killed 7 adult males and 17 infants from the bigger one in the course of battles fought over multiple years. The military success of the smaller faction in these encounters is puzzling, and the inability of the larger one to mount a more robust defense is puzzling. 

All human warfare is merely an extension of the battles fought in our common ancestor with the chimps.
