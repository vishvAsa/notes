## Potato
The sweet potato is candidate for two potential introductions to India. While not yet confirmed, it is possible that the wide-voyaging Austronesians who reached South America eventually transmitted to Asia and this resulted in a pre-Columbian introduction to India. Things referred to as Aruka (>Alu) *might* have included such. There was however a second confirmed Portuguese transmission of the batata = sweet potato in the 1500s that is the precursor of the currently dominant cultivars.

## groundnuts
For groundnuts the evidence for a pre-Columbian transmission is weak. However, there is the intriguing possibility of the transmission of its distant African cousin the bambara groundnut.

## datura
I have covered this topic in some length before. By examining old Skt literature we can fairly accurately pinpoint the earliest references of dhattUra in the subcontinent. We believe it is texts like the kAmasUtra, sushruta and subsequently multiple H and bauddha tantra-s that followed them. It was used as a psychedelic in tAntrika prayoga-s, which probably continued to tradition of the psychedelic unmAda-s of the muni-s in the veda. However, there is no reference to dhattUra in the vaidika tradition.

## Pineapple, custard apple
I've seen some claims for hoysala pineapples, which would make it pre-Columbian candidate. I remain dubious but am open to more investigation. Much weaker case than custardapple or sItAphalam.
