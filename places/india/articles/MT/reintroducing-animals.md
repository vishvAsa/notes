It is good to see the cheetah make its comeback in the desh. Hope that holds.  I also suspect that reintroducing the even more remotely extinct (by human hands) ostrich might be a good addition to the ecosystem, especially in Guj and Raj. The Javan rhinoceros, in principle, would be a good addition to the vA~nga country, but it is in too precarious a situation to even think of reintroduction. There is a perverse part of me that wonders about bringing back the hippopotamus. But I guess nobody would think of that, given how much they reengineer riverine ecosystems and the density of human populations on riverbanks. I wonder if they have been reintroduced to Ambani's forest though. 

The extinction of the native Indian Hippo, the massive Hexaprotodon, at the hands of Homo sapiens (the peoples whom Reich calls "AASI") probably resulted in huge riverine ecological changes, given how much they modify wetland through their feeding, defecation-sprays and trampling.
