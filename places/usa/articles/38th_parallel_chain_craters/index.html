The 38th Parallel Chain Craters

Seven or eight circular structures, 3-17 km wide, stretch in a nearly straight line for 700 km across Kansas, Missouri, and Illinois, all dating roughly to the same Mississippian-Pennsylvanian window.

Rampino & Volk (1996) proposed a chain impact — a tidally disrupted bolide hitting sequentially, like Shoemaker-Levy 9 at Jupiter. Geologists have been skeptical.

Objections

The standard objections:

  1. Earth’s gravity is too weak for tidal disruption
  2. The ages don’t all overlap
  3. Some structures look volcanic

All three objections collapse under scrutiny.

Gravity

On gravity: Since 1996 we’ve learned that most small bodies — Itokawa, Ryugu, Bennu — are rubble piles held together only by self-gravity, with essentially zero tensile strength. The Roche limit for a rubble pile on a grazing Earth trajectory is well within reach.

More decisively: the Davy crater chain on the Moon is a confirmed existence proof of exactly this mechanism operating at one-sixth Earth’s gravity — a body that cannot possibly be accused of having too strong a gravitational field. The mechanism works at lunar gravity. It works at Earth gravity. The “too weak” objection was always implicitly assuming a monolithic impactor, an assumption now known to be wrong for most NEOs.

Ages

On the ages: The error bars are enormous — Crooked Creek is dated at 320 ± 80 Ma. Under the coincidence hypothesis, you’d expect the structures scattered across geological time. Instead they’re all clustered in the same Paleozoic window. The probability of 7-8 independent random structures all falling within ~100 Ma of each other is roughly (1/5)^6 ~ 1/15,000. The apparent non-overlap in dates is exactly what imprecise measurement of a simultaneous event looks like. The skeptics are penalizing the hypothesis for their own chronometers’ limitations.

Geometry

On the geometry: The probability of 7-8 independent circular structures randomly aligning within 50 km of a straight line across a continent is perhaps 1/10,000.

Combined, the coincidence hypothesis requires something like a 1-in-150-million shot. The chain impact hypothesis requires only that a rubble-pile NEO had a grazing encounter with Earth once in 500 million years — which given the known impact flux is not unlikely at all.

Catastrophism response

The resistance is sociological. Field geology developed a strong immune response to catastrophism — Velikovsky, flood geology, cosmic nonsense — and the antibodies fire on extraterrestrial explanations regardless of evidence quality, effectively equating “extraterrestrial cause” with “supernatural cause.” The Alvarez impact should have retired that reflex.

As Terry Pratchett observed: some people will always bet on the 999,999-to-one shot over the million-to-one shot, because the million-to-one shot sounds too dramatic. The math doesn’t care.

Geochronology

The remaining honest uncertainty is geochronology — tight simultaneous dates on the unconfirmed structures would close the case. That work should be done.
