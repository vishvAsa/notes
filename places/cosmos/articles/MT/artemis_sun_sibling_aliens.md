The Artemis flight is sobering in a way. This is the farthest a manned flight has ever gone. That is a big deal, but everything else in the hostile solar system is so much farther away; what to say of the Alpha Centauri system we are passing by and then the rest of the Milky Way? 

To put it in perspective and to show how much time matters: The sun was not close to Alpha Centauri or Sirius. We are just passing by them. The sun was born far from its current location in a cluster close to its sibling HD 162826, which is now a star 110 light years away in the constellation of Hercules. That's the distance they have drifted since their birth. It is a slightly brighter and bigger star; hence, if it has any planets around it, they would have to be farther than the Earth in orbit to support life. Right now, we don't have a clue if it has a planet or not.

Many Americans, in particular, claim to see aliens. However, this tells us that whatever they are seeing, they are unlikely to be physical interstellar travelers. More likely, it is a psycho-conscious phenomenon intersecting with something else.
